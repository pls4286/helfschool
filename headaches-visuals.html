The three primary headache types require fundamentally different treatments — accurate type identification directly determines whether treatment works.
What distinguishes the three types
📍
Location
TTH: bilateral (band-like, both sides) · Migraine: usually one-sided · Cluster: strictly unilateral periorbital (around the eye) or temporal (at the temple)
Location
💢
Severity
TTH: mild-moderate pressing pain · Migraine: moderate-severe pulsating · Cluster: severe to very severe — rated among the most painful conditions known
Severity
⏱️
Duration
TTH: 30 min–7 days · Migraine: 4–72 hours · Cluster: 15 min–3 hours but up to 8 attacks per day — brief duration rules out oral analgesics
Duration
🤢
Associated features
TTH: none · Migraine: nausea, photophobia (light sensitivity), phonophobia (sound sensitivity) · Cluster: same-side tearing, nasal congestion, drooping eyelid
Features
🏃
Behaviour during attack
TTH: unaffected by activity · Migraine: worsened by activity — patient lies still · Cluster: restless, pacing — patient cannot keep still
Behaviour
⚠️
Medication overuse headache
A fourth pattern: daily headache from overusing acute treatments. Triptans or opioids >10 days/month, or analgesics >15 days/month — worsens with more medication
>10 days/mo
Treatment per headache type
💊
TTH — simple analgesia
Aspirin, ibuprofen, or paracetamol. Effective and appropriate. Triptans are NOT effective for TTH — this distinction matters clinically.
Analgesia
🚫
TTH — not triptans
Triptans are migraine-specific. Using them for TTH provides no benefit and risks developing medication overuse headache with frequent use.
No triptans
🎯
Migraine — triptan + NSAID early
Sumatriptan (Imigran) + NSAID combination as first-line per NICE CG150. Treatment at attack onset produces significantly better outcomes than waiting.
Early + combo
💨
Cluster — 100% oxygen
12–15 litres per minute for 15–20 minutes via non-rebreathable mask. Level A evidence. No systemic effects. The only primary headache treated with oxygen.
O₂ first
💉
Cluster — subcutaneous sumatriptan
6mg subcutaneous injection — alongside oxygen as first-line. Oral triptans are too slow for cluster attack duration. Verapamil (Cordilox) for prevention.
6mg SC
🔄
MOH — withdraw the drug
Stopping the overused medication is the recognised treatment. Headache initially worsens before improving. Around half achieve meaningful improvement within 2 months.
Withdraw

Subcutaneous sumatriptan for acute cluster headache
Sumatriptan Cluster Headache Study Group · N Engl J Med · 1991;325:322–326
Randomised double-blind crossover trial in 49 patients with cluster headache. Subcutaneous sumatriptan 6mg reduced headache severity in 74% of attacks within 15 minutes versus 26% on placebo; 46% were pain-free at 15 minutes versus 10% on placebo. The trial established subcutaneous sumatriptan as first-line acute treatment for cluster headache alongside 100% oxygen.

Global burden of migraine and tension-type headache
GBD 2016 Headache Collaborators · Lancet Neurol · 2018;17:954–976
The GBD 2016 headache-specific systematic analysis found approximately 1.89 billion people globally had tension-type headache — making it the most prevalent neurological condition worldwide. Despite this, TTH generates proportionally fewer disability-adjusted life years than migraine, because individual attacks are less severe. Over half the global adult population has an active headache disorder at any one time.
Headache patterns clinically recognised as requiring prompt assessment
⚡
Thunderclap headache
Reaches maximum intensity within seconds to minutes — clinically recognised as time-sensitive to exclude subarachnoid haemorrhage (bleeding around the brain).
Urgent
👴
New headache over 50
Particularly if progressive, or with scalp tenderness or jaw pain on chewing — warrants assessment to exclude giant cell arteritis (GCA — a vasculitic condition affecting arteries).
Assess
🌡️
Fever + neck stiffness ± rash
A pattern clinically recognised as time-sensitive — 111 and urgent GP services exist for this presentation. May indicate meningitis or other serious infection.
Time-sensitive
🧠
Focal neurological deficit
New weakness, speech difficulty, or visual loss not consistent with typical migraine aura — clinically recognised as requiring urgent assessment.
Urgent
💪
Worsened by exertion or straining
Headache aggravated by coughing, straining, or exertion (Valsalva manoeuvre — a bearing-down action) may indicate raised intracranial pressure (pressure inside the skull).
Investigate
🔬
Known cancer or immunosuppression
New or changing headache in someone with known cancer, HIV, or who is immunosuppressed warrants a lower threshold for investigation.
Lower threshold
