Segment 1 — What is MASLD?
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read
Screen 2: MASLD spectrum diagram · steatosis → MASH → fibrosis → cirrhosis → HCC

"MASLD stands for Metabolic dysfunction-Associated Steatotic Liver Disease. That's the new name for what most people call a fatty liver — previously known as NAFLD. The name changed in 2023. If you've been told you have a fatty liver or NAFLD, this is the same condition."

"It means that excess fat has accumulated in the liver — in people who drink little or no alcohol. The liver isn't supposed to store significant amounts of fat. When it does, it creates a spectrum of disease — from completely silent and reversible at one end, to serious liver damage at the other." PAUSE

"The spectrum goes: simple steatosis — fat in the liver, no inflammation, often reversible. Then MASH — that's where inflammation and cell damage begin. Then fibrosis — scarring. Then cirrhosis — advanced scarring that's largely irreversible. And in some cases, liver cancer."

"The key causes are obesity — especially abdominal fat, type 2 diabetes, insulin resistance, high triglycerides, and a Western diet high in ultra-processed food and added sugar. PAUSE And importantly — you can have MASLD at a normal weight. A healthy BMI doesn't rule it out."

"Waist circumference is a better measure of visceral fat — the dangerous kind — than BMI. Risk increases above 94cm in men and 80cm in women. If your waist is more than half your height, that's a clinically meaningful signal."

Segment 2 — Why does it matter?
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read
Screen 3: Stat cards · 1 in 5 UK · ~10 million · most undiagnosed

"MASLD affects around 1 in 5 people in the UK — approximately 10 million people. The vast majority have no symptoms. No pain. No warning signs. The liver has no pain receptors." PAUSE

"This is a condition that, if identified early, is often completely reversible. But if left undetected, it can progress quietly — over years or decades — to cirrhosis or liver cancer."

"MASLD is now the leading cause of chronic liver disease in the UK and the developed world. And it's increasing — driven by rising rates of obesity, type 2 diabetes, and ultra-processed food consumption in every age group."

"It also significantly increases the risk of cardiovascular disease — which remains the leading cause of death in people with MASLD. Managing fatty liver and managing cardiovascular risk are largely the same conversation." PAUSE

"And the good news: the evidence for lifestyle change is robust and consistent. Weight loss, dietary improvement, and exercise can reduce liver fat, improve inflammation, and even reverse early fibrosis. This is a condition where what you do genuinely matters."

Segment 3 — What your doctor will do
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read
Screen 4: Weight loss thresholds · 5% / 7% / 10% ladder

"MASLD is often picked up incidentally — on an ultrasound done for another reason, or when a blood test shows raised liver enzymes. Many GPs now screen people with known risk factors: obesity, type 2 diabetes, or metabolic syndrome."

"The key question isn't just do you have a fatty liver — it's how serious is it? Specifically, is there significant fibrosis — scarring? The guidelines recommend a two-step approach." PAUSE

"Step one: a FIB-4 score — a simple calculation from routine blood tests. Age, two liver enzyme measurements, and platelet count. Score below 1.3 — low risk, manage in primary care. Score above 2.67 — specialist referral. In between — go to step two."

"Step two: transient elastography — that's a FibroScan. A non-invasive scan that measures liver stiffness — a proxy for fibrosis. Avoids biopsy in most cases." PAUSE

"Treatment: lifestyle intervention is the cornerstone. There's currently no licensed drug specifically for MASLD in the UK. Weight loss targets are clear — 5% reduces liver fat. 7% reduces inflammation. 10% can reverse fibrosis. These are powerful numbers."

"Semaglutide (Ozempic, Wegovy) and other GLP-1 receptor agonists are showing significant promise in trials and may be prescribed for their licensed indications — obesity or type 2 diabetes — with liver benefit as an additional effect. Resmetirom (Rezdiffra) is approved in the USA and a NICE appraisal is awaited."

Segment 4 — What the research shows
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read
Screen 5: Research cards · EASL 2024 · weight loss thresholds · Med diet 39% · coffee evidence

"The current international standard for MASLD management is the EASL/EASD/EASO joint clinical practice guidelines, published in the Journal of Hepatology in June 2024. These cover everything from FIB-4 assessment in primary care through to pharmacological treatment for advanced disease."

"On weight loss — multiple randomised trials confirm: ≥5% reduces steatosis, ≥7% improves inflammation, ≥10% stabilises or reverses fibrosis — even at F2–F3 stage. The challenge is sustaining the weight loss." PAUSE

"On diet — a 12-week RCT found the Mediterranean diet reduced liver fat by 39% and improved insulin sensitivity. Mediterranean pattern consistently outperforms other diets in MASLD trials — through its effects on insulin resistance, inflammation, and gut microbiome."

"On coffee — genuinely evidence-based. Multiple studies associate regular coffee consumption — 2 to 3 cups per day — with slower progression of liver disease. The EASL 2024 guidelines explicitly recommend it. Anti-inflammatory and antifibrotic effects from the bioactive compounds." PAUSE

"UK prevalence data from the British Liver Trust 2025 — approximately 10 million people in the UK have MASLD. Only around 5.2 million diagnosed. A diagnosis gap of nearly half. This is an enormous public health opportunity."
