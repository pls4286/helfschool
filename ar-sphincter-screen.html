How Acid Reflux Happens — The Lower Oesophageal Sphincter
✓ Normal
✗ GORD

🔒 What the LOS does

Ring of muscle at the base of the gullet. Opens when you swallow to let food through, then closes tightly to keep stomach acid where it belongs.

🔥 What goes wrong in GORD

The LOS fails to close properly — stomach acid leaks back up into the oesophagus. The lining isn't designed for acid, so it becomes inflamed.

💡 Why it causes heartburn

Burning behind the breastbone — acid irritating the oesophageal lining. Despite the name, nothing to do with the heart.
