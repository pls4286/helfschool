The Gut Lining in Coeliac Disease
How gluten damages the villi — and what happens when gluten is removed
Healthy gut lining
No gluten exposure · normal absorption
Tall finger-like projections
Absorbing surface ≈ a tennis court
Iron, calcium, folate, vitamin D absorbed normally
Absorption capacity: 0%
Coeliac disease — gluten present
Immune attack flattens the lining
Villi shrink and flatten (villous atrophy)
Absorbing surface collapses toward a dining table
Nutrients pass through unabsorbed
Absorption capacity: 100%
Remove gluten → immune attack stops → villi recover → absorption restores. This is why the gluten-free diet works — and why it must be strict and lifelong.
