How bloating actually happens
🫧
Gas production
Bacteria ferment undigested FODMAPs in the colon → hydrogen, CO₂, methane.
🔄
Impaired transit
Gas doesn't move through efficiently — constipation and slow motility cause pressure to build.
🧠
Visceral hypersensitivity
Oversensitive gut nerves — normal gas feels painful. A central mechanism in IBS.
🦠
SIBO
Small intestinal bacterial overgrowth — bloating starts within minutes of eating.
The six main causes of bloating
🫧
Excess gas production
Bacteria ferment undigested carbs (FODMAPs) in the colon — producing hydrogen, CO₂ and methane.
→ Gas
🔄
Impaired gas transit
Gas doesn't move through efficiently. Slow motility and constipation cause pressure to build.
→ Pressure
🧠
Visceral hypersensitivity
Oversensitive gut nerves — normal gas feels painful. Central mechanism in IBS (irritable bowel syndrome).
→ Pain
🦠
SIBO — small intestinal bacterial overgrowth
Bacteria in the small intestine fermenting food too early — bloating starts minutes after eating.
→ Early onset
🥛
Food intolerances
Lactose, fructose, and other poorly absorbed sugars fermented by gut bacteria.
→ Trigger
⚖️
Microbiome imbalance
Individual differences in gut bacteria determine how much gas is produced from fermentation.
→ Variance
FODMAPs — what to trial-exclude & what to eat instead
🔴 High FODMAP — trial-exclude
🟢 Low FODMAP — well tolerated
🥛 Dairy
Cow's milk, yoghurt, soft cheese, ice cream
Lactose-free milk, hard cheese (cheddar), butter, brie
🍞 Grains
Wheat bread, rye, barley, wheat pasta, most cereals
Rice, oats, quinoa, gluten-free bread & pasta, corn
🧅 Vegetables
Onion, garlic, leek, asparagus, cauliflower, mushrooms
Carrot, tomato, courgette, spinach, potato, pepper
🍎 Fruit
Apple, pear, mango, watermelon, cherry, dried fruit
Banana (firm), strawberry, blueberry, orange, kiwi
🫘 Legumes
Baked beans, lentils, chickpeas, kidney beans
Canned rinsed chickpeas (small), firm tofu, tempeh
🍯 Sweeteners
Honey, sorbitol, mannitol, xylitol, fructose
Sugar (small), maple syrup (small), glucose, stevia
Restricted 2–6 weeks · then reintroduced one food at a time · ideally with a registered dietitian.
What your doctor might do — stepped approach
🧪
Targeted investigations first
Blood tests (coeliac tTG, FBC, thyroid) · hydrogen breath test (lactose, fructose, SIBO) · pelvic ultrasound for new bloating in women over 50.
1st-line
🥗
Dietary approach — dietitian-led low-FODMAP
Dietitian-led low-FODMAP trial. Lactose exclusion 2–4 weeks. Constipation addressed — fibre, hydration, activity.
Dietitian
💊
Medications — selected cases
Antispasmodics: mebeverine (Colofac) · low-dose amitriptyline for gut-brain modulation · rifaximin (Xifaxanta) where SIBO confirmed.
Targeted Rx
🧠
Gut–brain therapies
Gut-directed hypnotherapy · CBT (cognitive behavioural therapy) for IBS — particularly where anxiety or the gut-brain axis is involved.
Psych-gut
⚠️
Coeliac testing requires gluten in the diet
Coeliac testing requires gluten in the diet at the time of testing — starting gluten-free beforehand gives a false-negative result and the diagnosis can be missed.
Critical
What the evidence shows · prevalence & diet
Key Finding

Rome Foundation — global bloating survey
Rome Foundation · 2023 · 51,425 people · 26 countries
Nearly 18% experience bloating at least once a week. Women roughly twice as likely as men. Strongly associated with abdominal pain — and significantly underscreened in primary care ⚑.
Key Finding
↓ Bloating

low-FODMAP crossover RCT · Monash 2014
Halmos et al. — landmark low-FODMAP RCT
Gastroenterology · 2014 · Monash University · 30 IBS patients
Crossover trial. Low-FODMAP significantly reduced bloating, pain and wind compared with normal diet. The evidence-based first-line dietary treatment for IBS bloating ⚑.

Black, Staudacher & Ford — network meta-analysis
Gut · 2022 · 13 RCTs · 944 patients
Low-FODMAP ranked first for global symptoms, pain, and specifically bloating and distension. Strongest dietary evidence for bloating relief across all interventions studied ⚑.

What the evidence shows
Bloating has causes.
Most are treatable.
Targeted investigation and dietitian-led diet work resolves the vast majority of persistent bloating.
🩺

In UK practice, targeted investigations identify coeliac disease, SIBO, and food intolerances — all of which have specific treatments
