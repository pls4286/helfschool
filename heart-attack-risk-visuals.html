Heart attack risk —
and how it's estimated
How doctors calculate 10-year cardiovascular risk, what QRISK3 captures, and what moves the number.
Dr Paul · Retired NHS GP · helf.school
How UK doctors estimate 10-year cardiovascular risk
12%
A QRISK3 score of 12%
represents roughly a 12 in 100 chance of a heart attack or stroke in the next 10 years — based on age, sex, blood pressure, cholesterol, smoking, BMI, and medical history.
<10%
Lower risk
Lifestyle focus — regular monitoring continues
10–19%
Intermediate
Statin part of clinical discussion · NICE NG238
≥20%
High risk
Statin and lifestyle measures both strongly supported
Key risk factors: what QRISK3 captures — 1 of 2
📊
Age and sex
Risk approximately doubles every decade from midlife. Men tend to reach any given risk threshold around a decade earlier than women.
2× / decade
❤️
Blood pressure
Every 20 mmHg (millimetres of mercury — the standard pressure unit) rise in systolic blood pressure approximately doubles cardiovascular mortality risk across midlife.
20 mmHg = 2×
🩸
Cholesterol ratio
The ratio of total to HDL cholesterol captures LDL burden. Each 1 mmol/L rise in LDL is associated with increased major vascular event risk.
per mmol/L
Key risk factors: what QRISK3 captures — 2 of 2
🚬
Smoking status
Current smoking is associated with roughly two to four times higher cardiovascular risk — clinically recognised as the single most impactful modifiable factor.
2–4×
🧬
Diabetes and family history
Type 2 diabetes approximately doubles cardiovascular risk. A first-degree relative with premature heart disease (before age 60) is also a significant independent risk factor.
~2×
What your doctor might do — 1 of 2
🧮
QRISK3 calculation
Age, sex, BP, cholesterol, smoking, BMI, and comorbidities — combined into a personalised 10-year cardiovascular risk estimate.
Calculate
🩺
Blood pressure assessment
Clinic readings plus home or ambulatory monitoring — repeated readings over time, not a single result, confirm the clinical picture.
Check
🩸
Lipid panel and HbA1c
Non-fasting lipid panel plus HbA1c (a blood test reflecting average blood sugar over 3 months) to identify undiagnosed diabetes — both feed into the QRISK3 calculation.
Test
What your doctor might do — 2 of 2
📋
NHS Health Check
Free every 5 years for adults aged 40–74 in England — includes QRISK3 calculation, blood pressure, cholesterol, BMI and full lifestyle review.
Screen
🌿
Lifestyle measures
Mediterranean or DASH-style eating, regular aerobic activity, weight management, smoking cessation, and alcohol moderation — all clinically recognised as reducing cardiovascular risk.
Lifestyle
💊
Statin discussion — NICE NG238
At 10%+ 10-year risk, NICE NG238 identifies a high-intensity statin as the recommended first-line treatment for primary cardiovascular prevention.
Treat
Research · how well does QRISK3 work?
Key finding
Validated
in the largest UK primary care cardiovascular risk study
7.9 million UK primary care records · BMJ 2017 · Hippisley-Cox et al
QRISK3 — development and validation
Hippisley-Cox et al · BMJ · 2017 · n=7.9M
QRISK3 was validated on almost 8 million UK primary care records — more accurate than earlier tools, especially for people with mental illness or inflammatory conditions.
Key finding
Better
outcomes from risk-based vs single risk-factor treatment
overall risk approach identifies more who benefit · Collins and Altman · BMJ
Risk-based vs threshold-based care
Collins and Altman · BMJ · 2012
Treating people based on overall cardiovascular risk — rather than single cut-offs for blood pressure or cholesterol — identifies more who benefit and avoids overtreating those unlikely to do so.
Research · risk-based treatment evidence
Key finding
~22%
reduction in major cardiovascular events
per 1 mmol/L fall in LDL · CTT · Lancet 2010 · 26 trials · 170,000 participants
CTT — cholesterol lowering across risk levels
Baigent et al · The Lancet · 2010 · 26 trials
Every 1 mmol/L LDL reduction cut major vascular events by ~22% — consistent regardless of baseline cardiovascular risk, supporting risk-scaled prescribing across the full risk spectrum.
Key finding
~27%
reduction in stroke risk
per 10 mmHg fall in systolic BP · Ettehad · Lancet 2016 · n=613,815
Ettehad — blood pressure lowering
Ettehad et al · The Lancet · 2016 · n=613,815
Each 10 mmHg reduction in systolic blood pressure cut coronary heart disease by 17%, stroke by 27%, and major cardiovascular events by ~20% — independent of starting blood pressure level.
Research · most cardiovascular risk is modifiable
Key finding
~90%
of first MI risk attributable to modifiable factors
9 factors · 52 countries · INTERHEART · Lancet 2004 · n=29,972
INTERHEART — 52 countries · 29,972 participants
Yusuf et al · The Lancet · 2004
Nine modifiable factors — lipids, smoking, blood pressure, diabetes, obesity, diet, activity, alcohol, and psychosocial stress — accounted for ~90% of first heart attack risk across all ages and regions.
Key finding
~50%
lower coronary event rate with favourable lifestyle
even in highest genetic predisposition · Khera · NEJM 2016 · n≈55,685
Lifestyle and genetic risk — Khera et al
Khera et al · NEJM · 2016 · n≈55,685
Even in those with the highest genetic predisposition to coronary disease, a favourable lifestyle was associated with ~50% lower event rates — lifestyle powerfully modifies inherited risk.
How risk factors drive heart attack — 1 of 2
💪
Pressure stress — endothelial injury
Elevated blood pressure creates constant shear stress on artery walls — damaging the endothelium (inner lining) and initiating the atherosclerosis cascade.
Endothelial injury
🩸
LDL infiltration and oxidation
Raised LDL penetrates the damaged artery wall, oxidises, and is engulfed by immune cells — forming the fatty core of atherosclerotic plaque.
Plaque
🚬
Smoking — toxin damage and clot promotion
Tobacco chemicals injure the endothelium, raise fibrinogen (a clotting protein), and promote clot formation on already-vulnerable plaques.
Clot risk
How risk factors drive heart attack — 2 of 2
🍬
Diabetes — glycation and arterial stiffening
Elevated blood glucose glycates proteins, stiffens arterial walls, and accelerates every stage of the atherosclerotic process.
Accelerated ageing
⚖️
Obesity — visceral fat and inflammation
Excess visceral fat (fat stored around internal organs) secretes inflammatory proteins and worsens blood pressure, lipids, and insulin resistance together.
Inflammation
💥
Plaque rupture — clot and occlusion
Risk factors converge on a final event: an unstable plaque tears, a clot rapidly forms, and the coronary artery becomes blocked — a myocardial infarction.
MI
