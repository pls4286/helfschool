Key risk factors: what QRISK3 counts
📊
Age & sex
Risk roughly doubles every decade from midlife. Men tend to reach any given risk threshold earlier than women.
2× / decade
❤️
Blood pressure
Every 20 mmHg rise in systolic BP roughly doubles cardiovascular mortality across midlife.
20 mmHg = 2×
🩸
Cholesterol ratio
Total / HDL ratio captures LDL burden. Each 1 mmol/L rise in LDL raises major vascular events.
per mmol/L
🚬
Smoking status
Current smokers have roughly two to four times higher CV risk. Stopping is the single biggest modifiable factor.
2–4×
🧬
Diabetes & family history
Type 2 diabetes roughly doubles CV risk. A first-degree relative with early MI also adds substantially.
~2×
What your doctor might do
🧮
QRISK3 calculation
Age, sex, BP, cholesterol, smoking, BMI, comorbidities — combined into a 10-year CV risk estimate.
→ Calculate
🩺
Blood pressure
Clinic and home / ambulatory readings — repeated visits, not a single reading, confirms diagnosis.
→ Check
🩸
Lipid panel + HbA1c
Non-fasting lipid panel and HbA1c to pick up undiagnosed diabetes — both feed straight into the risk score.
→ Test
📋
NHS Health Check
Free every 5 years for adults aged 40–74 — includes QRISK assessment, BP, cholesterol, BMI and lifestyle review.
→ Screen
🌿
Lifestyle guidance
Mediterranean / DASH-style diet, regular activity, weight management, smoking cessation, alcohol moderation.
→ Advise
💊
Statin discussion
At 10%+ 10-year risk NICE NG238 recommends a conversation about starting atorvastatin (Lipitor) 20 mg.
→ Treat
Research · how well does QRISK3 work?
Validated
discrimination & calibration · UK cohort
QRISK3 development & validation
Hippisley-Cox et al · BMJ · 2017 · n=7.9M
QRISK3 was derived and validated on almost 8 million UK primary care records. It predicts CV events more accurately than earlier tools, including newer risk factors like migraine, SLE and severe mental illness.
Better
than single risk-factor approaches
Risk-based vs threshold-based care
Collins & Altman · BMJ · 2012 & follow-up
Treating people based on overall calculated risk — rather than single cut-offs for BP or cholesterol — identifies more of those who benefit and avoids treating those who don't.
Research · risk-based treatment benefits
−22%
major events · per 1 mmol/L ↓ LDL
CTT — cholesterol lowering
Baigent et al · The Lancet · 2010 · 26 trials
Every 1 mmol/L reduction in LDL produced a ~22% reduction in major vascular events — the benefit held regardless of baseline risk, supporting risk-scaled prescribing.
−20 to −27%
CV events · per 10 mmHg ↓ systolic BP
Ettehad — BP lowering
Ettehad et al · The Lancet · 2016 · n=613,815
Each 10 mmHg reduction in systolic BP cut CHD by 17%, stroke by 27%, and major cardiovascular events by about 20% — independent of starting level.
Research · most risk is modifiable
~90%
MI risk · attributable to 9 modifiable factors
INTERHEART — 52 countries
Yusuf et al · The Lancet · 2004 · n=29,972
Across 52 countries, nine modifiable factors — lipids, smoking, BP, diabetes, obesity, diet, activity, alcohol, psychosocial — accounted for about 90% of the population risk of a first heart attack.
−50%
CHD · favourable lifestyle · high genetic risk
Lifestyle × genetic risk
Khera et al · NEJM · 2016 · n≈55,685
Even in people with the highest genetic risk, a favourable lifestyle (not smoking, not obese, regular activity, healthy diet) was associated with a roughly 50% lower rate of coronary events.
How risk factors drive heart attack
💪
Pressure stress
High BP creates constant shear stress on the artery wall — endothelial damage starts the atherosclerosis cascade.
→ Endothelial injury
🩸
LDL infiltration
Raised LDL deposits into the damaged wall, oxidises, and is engulfed by macrophages — forming atherosclerotic plaque.
→ Plaque
🚬
Smoking toxins
Thousands of chemicals injure endothelium, raise fibrinogen, and promote clot formation on vulnerable plaques.
→ Clot risk
🍬
Diabetes / glycation
High glucose glycates proteins, stiffens arteries, and accelerates every stage of atherosclerosis.
→ Accelerated ageing
⚖️
Obesity & inflammation
Excess visceral fat secretes inflammatory cytokines and worsens BP, lipids, and insulin resistance together.
→ Inflammation
💥
Plaque rupture + clot
Risk factors converge on one final event — an unstable plaque tears, a clot forms, and the coronary artery blocks.
→ MI
