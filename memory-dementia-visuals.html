Dementia is not one disease — it is an umbrella term
Five main types, distinguished by their cause, pattern and typical presentation
🧠 Alzheimer's disease ~65%
Amyloid plaques and tau tangles damage nerve cells progressively. Memory for recent events is typically the earliest symptom. The most common form.
🫀 Vascular dementia ~20%
Reduced blood supply to the brain — often following stroke or TIA, or from gradual small vessel disease. May worsen in steps rather than steadily.
👁️ Dementia with Lewy bodies
Protein deposits in nerve cells. Characterised by visual hallucinations, Parkinsonism (tremor and stiffness), and fluctuating alertness — often misdiagnosed.
💬 Frontotemporal dementia
Affects personality, behaviour and language before memory. Most common form in people under 65. Changes can be subtle and easily misattributed to stress.
🔀 Mixed dementia
Two or more types coexisting — most commonly Alzheimer's alongside vascular dementia. More common than previously recognised, particularly in older adults. Diagnosis now better supported by brain imaging and biomarkers.

Hearing loss — the single largest modifiable risk factor
Source: Livingston et al, Lancet 2024
9%
of dementia cases globally are linked to untreated hearing loss
More than any other single modifiable risk factor
🧩
Cognitive load
Struggling to hear forces the brain to use extra resources to process sound — resources that would otherwise support memory, attention and reasoning.
🧠
Brain structure
Reduced auditory input is associated with changes in brain structure over time, including accelerated shrinkage in areas important for memory and cognition.
🤝
Social withdrawal
Untreated hearing loss drives social isolation — itself an independent dementia risk factor. Hearing aids appear to reduce risk, particularly in people with multiple risk factors.
Assessment and management in UK practice
NICE NG97 — Dementia: assessment, management and support (2018, reviewed 2025)
📋
Cognitive assessment
Standardised tests (MoCA, 6-CIT, MMSE) assess memory, attention, language and reasoning — providing a structured, repeatable baseline to track change over time.
MoCA · 6-CIT
🩸
Blood tests — exclude reversible causes
Thyroid function, B12, folate, glucose, calcium. An underactive thyroid or B12 deficiency can cause significant cognitive slowing — both entirely treatable.
Reversible
🔬
Brain imaging & memory clinic
MRI or CT to exclude treatable structural causes and identify dementia subtype. Referral to memory services for formal neuropsychological assessment and diagnosis.
MRI · CT
💊
Medications — Alzheimer's disease
Cholinesterase inhibitors (donepezil/Aricept, rivastigmine/Exelon, galantamine/Reminyl) modestly slow symptom progression. Memantine/Ebixa for moderate-severe disease. Symptomatic — not curative.
Symptomatic
📝
Post-diagnosis planning
Lasting power of attorney — a legal document allowing a trusted person to make decisions if capacity is lost. DVLA notification. Care planning. Connection to Alzheimer's Society and Admiral Nurses.
LPA · DVLA
What the research shows — prevention
Livingston et al, Lancet 2024
Lancet Commission 2024
45%
of dementia cases are potentially preventable
14 modifiable risk factors · acts across the full life course · never too early or too late

The 2024 Lancet Commission on Dementia Prevention, Intervention and Care — the world's most comprehensive review of dementia risk — concludes that 45% of future dementia cases could be prevented or delayed by addressing 14 modifiable risk factors. Two new factors were added in 2024: high LDL cholesterol in midlife (7%) and untreated vision loss in later life. The life-course framework is central: risk starts accumulating in childhood, but action at any life stage meaningfully reduces risk. Age-specific dementia incidence is already declining in high-income countries — evidence that prevention is working in the real world.

Livingston G et al. Lancet. 2024;404(10452):572–628. doi:10.1016/S0140-6736(24)01296-0.

9%

The largest single modifiable risk factor · hearing aids reduce the risk

Hearing loss in midlife carries the highest individual population-attributable fraction of any modifiable risk factor for dementia at 9%. The 2024 Commission states the evidence that treating hearing loss reduces dementia risk is now stronger than in 2020. People who use hearing aids have significantly lower rates of dementia, with the benefit most marked in those with multiple risk factors. The mechanism involves cognitive load, brain structural changes, and the role of hearing loss in driving social isolation.

Livingston G et al. Lancet. 2024;404(10452):572–628.
What the research shows — the scale in the UK
Alzheimer's Research UK analysis of ONS, NRS and NISRA data, 2025 · Carnall Farrar / Alzheimer's Society, 2024
UK burden 2024
UK's biggest killer
Dementia is the leading cause of death in the UK
76,894 deaths in 2024 · 1 in 8 of all UK deaths · leading cause for women since 2011

Analysis of official death registration data across all four UK nations confirmed dementia as the UK's leading cause of death in 2024, claiming 76,894 lives — 11.8% of all registered deaths that year. Dementia has been the leading cause of death for UK women since 2011 and remains second to heart disease in men. Unlike most major conditions, no treatment currently available on the NHS can stop or reverse the disease process — making prevention the most impactful intervention available.

Alzheimer's Research UK analysis of ONS, NRS & NISRA data. Published December 2025.
Projection
1.4M
people projected to have dementia in UK by 2040
Up from 982,000 today · £90bn projected annual cost by 2040

Approximately 982,000 people in the UK are currently living with dementia — a figure projected to rise to 1.4 million by 2040 as the population ages. The annual economic cost was £42 billion in 2024, projected to reach £90 billion by 2040. Families bear approximately 63% of the total cost through unpaid caring. These projections are the central reason why dementia prevention has become one of the highest priorities in UK public health — and why the 45% preventable fraction from the Lancet Commission is among the most significant findings in modern public health research.

Carnall Farrar. The Economic Impact of Dementia. Report commissioned by Alzheimer's Society. 2024.
