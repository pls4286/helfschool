Why it matters: five body systems — 1 of 2
🧬
Cancer
Mouth, throat, oesophagus, liver, bowel, and female breast. IARC Group 1 carcinogen — same category as tobacco — since 1988.
7 types
❤️
Heart
Raises blood pressure and triggers atrial fibrillation (AF — irregular heartbeat). No protective dose — risk rises with every unit.
1.3×
🩸
Liver
75% of UK alcohol-specific deaths are from liver disease. Progresses: fatty liver → hepatitis → cirrhosis.
75%
Why it matters: five body systems — 2 of 2
🧠
Brain
Grey and white matter loss detectable from 1–2 units per day. Raises long-term dementia risk independently of other factors.
1 unit
💭
Mood & sleep
Fragments sleep, flattens mood, weakens antidepressants. Raises suicide risk at higher intakes. Feels like it helps — the data says otherwise.
↓ Sleep
What your doctor might do — 1 of 2
📋
AUDIT-C screen
Three quick questions — frequency, typical amount, binge episodes. Score ≥5 triggers full assessment.
→ Screen
🩸
Blood tests
LFTs (liver function tests), GGT, and red-cell size (MCV) pick up chronic heavy use. Social drinking often looks normal.
→ Test
🔬
Liver assessment
FibroScan measures liver scarring without a biopsy. Ultrasound identifies fatty liver; hepatology referral for advanced cases.
→ Scan
What your doctor might do — 2 of 2
💬
Brief intervention
Short structured conversation — personal risk, motivation, practical goals. Cochrane-reviewed and evidence-backed for hazardous drinking.
→ Guide
⚕️
NICE CG115 pathway
CBT, medications for alcohol dependence, and supervised withdrawal with thiamine cover — for clinically confirmed dependence.
→ Treat
🤝
Community support
Local ICB services, Alcoholics Anonymous, SMART Recovery, Alcohol Change UK, and online self-help tools.
→ Support

Key Finding
2.8M
alcohol-attributable deaths globally
2016 · level minimising overall health risk: zero
GBD 2016 — 195 countries
Griswold et al · The Lancet · 2018
7th leading risk factor globally. Headline finding: the level that minimises overall health risk is zero.
Key Finding
1.34B
people drinking harmful amounts
2020 · under-40s: lowest-risk intake near zero
GBD 2020 — age-specific
Bryazka et al · The Lancet · 2022
For adults under 40 → lowest-risk intake is near zero. For over-40s → small intake may be net-neutral for some, but not protective at scale.
Research · the "J-curve" debunked
Key Finding
1.3×
higher hypertension risk
per SD increase in alcohol · risk monotonic — no protective dose
Biddinger · UK Biobank MR
JAMA Network Open · 2022 · n=371,463
Mendelian randomisation — cardiovascular risk rises monotonically with alcohol. 1.4× coronary artery disease risk per SD increase.
Key Finding
107
cohort studies · 4.8M participants
after "sick quitter" correction — no mortality benefit at low-moderate intake
Zhao · all-cause mortality
JAMA Network Open · 2023
After correcting for "sick quitter" bias, light-to-moderate drinking showed no mortality benefit. Risk rises from 25g/day (women), 45g/day (men).
Research · brain and dementia
Key Finding
~2 yrs
brain-ageing effect
going from 1→2 units/day at age 50 — grey & white matter loss from 1 unit/day
Daviet · UK Biobank brain MRI
Nature Communications · 2022 · n=36,678
Grey & white matter volume losses detectable from 1–2 units/day. "Moderate" drinking is not neurologically neutral.
Key Finding
+17%
higher dementia risk
per 7 units above 14/week · Lancet Commission 2024 confirms alcohol as modifiable risk factor
Sabia · Whitehall II · 23-yr follow-up
BMJ · 2018 · n=9,087
Drinkers exceeding 14 units/week showed stepwise dementia rise. Confirmed in Lancet Commission 2024 as one of 14 modifiable risk factors.
How alcohol actually harms — 1 of 2
🧬
DNA damage
Acetaldehyde (alcohol's breakdown product) binds directly to DNA, causes mutations, and blocks DNA repair.
→ Cancer
🔥
Oxidative stress
Reactive oxygen species damage cells; chronic drinking drives systemic inflammation across organs.
→ Inflam.
💧
Hormone disruption
Raises circulating oestrogen and lowers sex hormone-binding globulin — key pathway in female breast cancer.
→ Breast
How alcohol actually harms — 2 of 2
❤️
Cardiac irritation
Triggers atrial fibrillation directly; raises blood pressure acutely and chronically; weakens the heart muscle at high intakes.
→ AF · BP
🍽️
Gut barrier loss
Damages the gut lining — bacterial products cross into the blood and drive liver inflammation.
→ Liver
🧠
Neurotoxicity
Directly toxic to brain cells; blocks thiamine (vitamin B1) absorption — the cause of Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome.
→ Memory
What the evidence shows
Most benefit comes from
drinking less than you do now.
🧬
Alcohol causes 7 cancers — IARC Group 1 carcinogen, same category as tobacco
📉
The level minimising overall health risk is zero — GBD 2016, 195 countries
🧠
Grey and white matter loss is detectable from 1–2 units per day
❤️
Cardiovascular risk rises monotonically — no protective dose confirmed by Mendelian randomisation
🗓️
Three or more drink-free days each week — measurable cardiovascular and liver benefit
📏
Smaller measures and lower-ABV choices reduce weekly units without changing social patterns
