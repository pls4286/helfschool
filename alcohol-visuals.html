Why it matters: five body systems
🧬
Cancer
7 types
Mouth, throat, oesophagus, liver, bowel, female breast — IARC Group 1 carcinogen since 1988.
🫀
Heart & circulation
↑ BP · AF
Genetics show risk rises monotonically with intake — no protective dose for CV disease.
🫁
Liver
3 stages
Steatosis → hepatitis → cirrhosis. 75% of UK alcohol-specific deaths are from liver disease.
🧠
Brain
Shrinks
Grey & white matter loss detectable from 1–2 units/day. Raises dementia risk long-term.
💭
Mood & sleep
Worsens
Fragments sleep, flattens mood, weakens antidepressants, raises suicide risk.
What your doctor might do
📋AUDIT-C screen
Three questions — frequency, typical amount, binge episodes. Score ≥5 = fuller assessment.
🩸Blood tests
LFTs, GGT, FBC with MCV — pick up chronic heavy drinking, not social use.
🔬Liver assessment
FibroScan or ultrasound if concern. Hepatology referral for advanced scarring.
💬Brief intervention
Short structured conversation — risk, motivation, practical goals. Cochrane-backed.
⚕️NICE CG115 pathway
CBT, acamprosate, naltrexone, supervised withdrawal with thiamine cover.
🤝Community support
Local ICB services, Alcoholics Anonymous, SMART Recovery, Alcohol Change UK.

For adults under 40 → lowest-risk intake is near zero. For over-40s → small intake may be net-neutral for some, but not protective at scale.
Research · the "J-curve" debunked
1.3×
HTN risk per SD increase in intake
Biddinger · UK Biobank MR
JAMA Network Open · 2022 · n=371,463
Mendelian randomisation (genes as natural experiment) — cardiovascular risk rises monotonically with alcohol. 1.4× CAD risk per SD increase.
107
cohort studies · 4.8M participants
Zhao · all-cause mortality
JAMA Network Open · 2023
After correcting for "sick quitter" bias, light-to-moderate drinking showed no mortality benefit. Risk rises from 25g/day (W), 45g/day (M).
Research · brain and dementia
~2 yrs
brain-ageing effect · 1→2 units/day at age 50
Daviet · UK Biobank brain MRI
Nature Communications · 2022 · n=36,678
Grey & white matter volume losses detectable from 1–2 units/day. "Moderate" drinking is not neurologically neutral.
+17%
dementia risk per 7-unit rise above 14/wk
Sabia · Whitehall II · 23-yr follow-up
BMJ · 2018 · n=9,087
Drinkers exceeding 14 units/week showed stepwise rise in dementia. Confirmed in Lancet Commission 2024 as one of 14 modifiable risk factors.
How alcohol actually harms
🧬DNA damage
Acetaldehyde (alcohol's breakdown product) binds to DNA and causes mutations — the main route to cancer.
🔥Oxidative stress
Reactive molecules damage cells; chronic drinking drives systemic inflammation across organs.
💧Hormone disruption
Raises oestrogen, lowers SHBG — key mechanism in female breast cancer.
🫀Cardiac irritation
Triggers atrial fibrillation; raises BP; weakens heart muscle at high intakes.
🍽️Gut barrier loss
Damages gut lining ("leaky gut") — drives liver inflammation and immune activation.
🧠Neurotoxicity
Directly toxic to neurons; blocks thiamine — cause of Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome.
Where to start
🌿
Most benefit comes from drinking less than you do now
Not perfect. Just less.
Blood pressure falls within weeks. Sleep improves within days. Fatty liver reverses within months. The curve is not all-or-nothing.
