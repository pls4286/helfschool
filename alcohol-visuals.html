Why it matters: five body systems
🧬
Cancer
Mouth, throat, oesophagus, liver, bowel, female breast. IARC Group 1 carcinogen since 1988.
7 types
❤️
Heart
Raises blood pressure and triggers atrial fibrillation. No protective dose — risk rises with every unit.
1.3×
🩸
Liver
75% of UK alcohol-specific deaths are from liver disease. Progresses: fatty liver → hepatitis → cirrhosis.
75%
🧠
Brain
Grey and white matter loss detectable from 1–2 units per day. Raises long-term dementia risk.
1 unit
💭
Mood & sleep
Fragments sleep, flattens mood, weakens antidepressants. Raises suicide risk at higher intakes.
↓ Sleep
What your doctor might do
📋
AUDIT-C screen
Three quick questions — frequency, typical amount, binge episodes. Score ≥5 triggers full assessment.
→ Screen
🩸
Blood tests
LFTs, GGT, and red-cell size (MCV) pick up chronic heavy use. Social drinking often looks normal.
→ Test
🔬
Liver assessment
FibroScan measures liver scarring without a biopsy. Ultrasound identifies fatty liver; hepatology referral for advanced cases.
→ Scan
💬
Brief intervention
Short structured conversation — personal risk, motivation, practical goals. Cochrane-reviewed and evidence-backed.
→ Guide
⚕️
NICE CG115 pathway
CBT, medications (acamprosate, naltrexone), supervised withdrawal with thiamine cover for dependence.
→ Treat
🤝
Community support
Local ICB services, Alcoholics Anonymous, SMART Recovery, Alcohol Change UK, online self-help tools.
→ Support

Key Finding

per SD increase in alcohol · risk monotonic — no protective dose
Biddinger · UK Biobank MR
JAMA Network Open · 2022 · n=371,463
Mendelian randomisation (genes as natural experiment) — cardiovascular risk rises monotonically with alcohol. 1.4× CAD risk per SD increase.
Key Finding
107

Zhao · all-cause mortality
JAMA Network Open · 2023
After correcting for "sick quitter" bias, light-to-moderate drinking showed no mortality benefit. Risk rises from 25g/day (W), 45g/day (M).
Research · brain and dementia
Key Finding
~2 yrs
brain-ageing effect
going from 1→2 units/day at age 50 — grey & white matter loss from 1 unit/day
Daviet · UK Biobank brain MRI
Nature Communications · 2022 · n=36,678
Grey & white matter volume losses detectable from 1–2 units/day. "Moderate" drinking is not neurologically neutral.
Key Finding
+17%
higher dementia risk
per 7 units above 14/week · Lancet Commission 2024 confirms alcohol as modifiable risk factor
Sabia · Whitehall II · 23-yr follow-up
BMJ · 2018 · n=9,087
Drinkers exceeding 14 units/week showed stepwise rise in dementia. Confirmed in Lancet Commission 2024 as one of 14 modifiable risk factors.
How alcohol actually harms
🧬
DNA damage
Acetaldehyde (alcohol's breakdown product) binds directly to DNA, causes mutations, and blocks DNA repair.
→ Cancer
🔥
Oxidative stress
Reactive oxygen species damage cells; chronic drinking drives systemic inflammation across organs.
→ Inflam.
💧
Hormone disruption
Raises circulating oestrogen and lowers sex hormone-binding globulin — key pathway in female breast cancer.
→ Breast
❤️
Cardiac irritation
Triggers atrial fibrillation directly; raises blood pressure acutely and chronically; weakens the heart muscle at high intakes.
→ AF · BP
🍽️
Gut barrier loss
Damages the gut lining ("leaky gut") — bacterial products cross into the blood and drive liver inflammation.
→ Liver
🧠
Neurotoxicity
Directly toxic to brain cells; blocks thiamine (vitamin B1) absorption — the cause of Wernicke-Korsakoff.
→ Memory
Where to start
🌿
Most benefit comes from
drinking less than you do now.
Blood pressure falls within weeks. Sleep improves within days.
🗓️
Drink-free days
Three or more alcohol-free days weekly — evidence shows measurable cardiovascular and liver benefit from this pattern alone.
3+
📏
Smaller measures
Lower volume per drink reduces weekly unit count without necessarily changing social habits. A 125ml glass vs 250ml halves the dose.
−½
🍺
Lower-ABV options
A 4% lager vs 5.5% reduces alcohol dose per drink by approximately 27%. Formula: volume × ABV ÷ 1000 = units.
−27%
🔢
Know the numbers
Unit counting is the most reliable method in UK practice for understanding actual weekly intake. Research shows most people underestimate by approximately half.
~½ off
