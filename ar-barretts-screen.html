📊 How common is this?

Most people with GORD never develop Barrett's. It affects approximately 10–15% of those with chronic, longstanding symptoms — and most Barrett's patients never develop cancer.

📉 Annual cancer risk in Barrett's

Approximately 0.33% per year — around 1 in 300 per year. This is much lower than earlier estimates. Risk is higher in those with longer Barrett's segments or dysplasia. (Desai TK et al., Gut 2012)

🏥 Why surveillance matters

The NHS offers endoscopic surveillance for people with confirmed Barrett's — typically every 2–5 years. Catching dysplasia early means treatment is far more effective and less invasive.
