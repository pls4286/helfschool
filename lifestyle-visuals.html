The big picture · lifestyle drives most CV risk

of first heart-attack risk
is attributable to nine modifiable factors — lipids, smoking, BP, diabetes, obesity, diet, activity, alcohol, psychosocial stress.

+10 yrs
CVD-free life
could be added by addressing five risk factors at age 50 — BP, cholesterol, obesity, diabetes, smoking.

Effects are additive — each lever moved adds further protection. All seven do not need to change to benefit.
What drives cardiovascular risk up — 1 of 2
🍔
Ultra-processed & high-sodium diets
High processed meat, sugary drinks, refined snacks — associated with higher CV events, independent of total calories.
↑ CV events
🛋️
Sedentary behaviour
Prolonged sitting raises all-cause and cardiovascular mortality independent of planned exercise.
↑ Mortality
🚬
Current smoking
Current smokers carry around 2–4 times the cardiovascular risk of never-smokers.
2–4×
What drives cardiovascular risk up — 2 of 2
⚖️
Excess weight · central adiposity
Visceral fat drives inflammation, insulin resistance and higher BP — central adiposity carries more risk than BMI alone.
BMI ≥30
🍷
Alcohol intake
Mendelian randomisation: genetically higher alcohol intake linked to higher hypertension and CHD — no evidence of a protective dose.
Linear harm
😴
Short or long sleep
J-shaped curve — both less than 6 and more than 9 hours per night independently associated with higher CV mortality.
<6 / >9 h
What moves the dial — evidence-based levers — 1 of 2
🥗
Mediterranean eating pattern
More veg, fruit, legumes, fish, olive oil; less processed and red meat. Direction, not rigid prescription.
−30%
🚶
Regular movement
Even 75 min/week moderate activity is associated with ∼17% lower CV risk — biggest gains from zero to some.
−17%
🚭
Stopping smoking
Cuts further CV event risk by ∼36% in existing CVD. Risk halves within a year; approaches non-smoker level over 15 years.
−36%
What moves the dial — evidence-based levers — 2 of 2
⚖️
5–10% weight reduction
Produces clinically meaningful reductions in BP, lipids, HbA1c and cardiovascular event risk.
5–10%
🍷
Lower alcohol intake
Current evidence does not support a “protective” moderate dose — for CV risk, less is consistently better.
Lower
😴
7–8 hours sleep
Lowest CV mortality sits at 7–8 hours per night in pooled data from 74 studies and over 3 million participants.
7–8 h
Research · how much is modifiable?
Key Finding
∼90%
of first heart-attack risk
from 9 modifiable factors — consistent across 52 countries
INTERHEART — 52 countries
Yusuf et al · The Lancet · 2004 · n=29,972
Nine modifiable factors accounted for ∼90% of population risk of a first heart attack. Psychosocial stress alone contributed ∼33% of attributable risk.
Key Finding
−50%
lower CHD event rate
in highest genetic risk group with favourable lifestyle
Lifestyle × genetic risk
Khera et al · NEJM · 2016 · n≈55,685
Even among those with the highest genetic risk, a favourable lifestyle — not smoking, healthy weight, regular activity, healthy diet — was associated with ∼50% lower CHD event rates.
Research · diet & activity
Key Finding
∼30%
fewer major CV events
Mediterranean diet with olive oil or nuts vs control — PREDIMED · n=7,447
PREDIMED — Mediterranean + olive oil / nuts
Estruch et al · NEJM · 2018 · n=7,447
Randomised to a Mediterranean diet with olive oil or nuts, high-risk adults had ∼30% fewer major cardiovascular events vs control over five years.
Key Finding
−17%
lower cardiovascular risk
at just 75 min/week moderate activity — half recommended level
Activity dose–response meta-analysis
Garcia et al · BJSM · 2023 · n≈30M
Even half the recommended activity — 75 min/week — was linked to ∼17% lower cardiovascular risk. Biggest gains came from moving from inactive to any regular movement.
Research · smoking & sleep
Key Finding
−36%
fewer further CV events
after stopping smoking in people with established CVD
Smoking cessation after CV events
Cochrane review · cardiovascular outcomes
In people with established CV disease, stopping smoking reduced further cardiovascular events by ∼36% — a larger effect than almost any single medication.
Key Finding
J-curve
sleep & CV mortality
lowest risk at 7–8 hrs — both <6 and >9 hrs raise risk
Sleep & CV mortality meta-analysis
Yin et al · JAHA · 2019 · n>3M
74 studies, over 3 million participants. Both short (<6–7h) and long (>8–9h) sleep were independently linked to higher CV mortality. Lowest risk clustered around 7–8 hours.
How lifestyle translates into heart protection — 1 of 2
🥗
Mediterranean pattern
Rich in polyphenols, fibre, unsaturated fats — lowers LDL, reduces inflammation, supports gut microbiome.
→ ↓ Inflammation
🚶
Regular movement
Lowers resting BP, raises HDL, improves insulin sensitivity, and trains the cardiovascular system day-on-day.
→ ↓ BP, ↑ HDL
🚭
Smoking cessation
Endothelial function improves within weeks. Carbon monoxide clears, fibrinogen falls, clot-risk normalises over years.
→ ↑ Endothelium
How lifestyle translates into heart protection — 2 of 2
⚖️
Weight reduction
Reduces visceral fat cytokines, improves BP, lipids, and glucose together — multiple risk factors move at once.
→ ↓ Cytokines
🍷
Lower alcohol
Less alcohol reduces BP, arrhythmia triggers (AF — atrial fibrillation), liver strain, and inflammatory markers over time.
→ ↓ BP, AF
😴
Protected sleep
Stabilises BP overnight, calms the HPA stress axis, and supports metabolic regulation and inflammation control.
→ ↓ Stress axis
