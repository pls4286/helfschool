Diet — the strongest evidence
PREDIMED Trial — NEJM 2018
7,447 people at high cardiovascular risk. Mediterranean diet supplemented with olive oil or nuts produced a ~30% reduction in major cardiovascular events vs control diet over ~5 years. The most robust dietary RCT evidence available.
Estruch R et al. NEJM 2018 (updated analysis)
Ultra-processed foods — BMJ 2024
Umbrella review of meta-analyses found consistent associations between ultra-processed food consumption and increased cardiovascular risk, independent of overall diet quality. Strongest effect: processed meat and sugar-sweetened beverages.
Lane MM et al. BMJ 2024

Smoking & Exercise — two of the most powerful levers
Smoking cessation — Cochrane review
Stopping smoking reduces risk of further cardiovascular event in those who have already had one by approximately 36% — a larger effect than almost any medication. Risk halves within 1 year. Approaches non-smoker level after 15 years.
Cochrane Database — Smoking cessation and CVD outcomes
Smoking timeline
1 year → excess risk halved
5 years → stroke risk approaches non-smoker
15 years → cardiovascular risk near non-smoker level
Physical activity — BJSM meta-analysis 2023
196 articles, 94 cohorts, 30 million+ participants. Just 75 min/week of moderate activity associated with a 17% reduction in CVD risk. Greatest gains seen moving from inactivity to even modest movement.
Garcia L et al. BJSM 2023

What the research shows — key studies
INTERHEART Study — The Lancet 2004
30,000+ people, 52 countries. Nine modifiable risk factors account for more than 90% of the risk of a first heart attack worldwide. Psychosocial stress alone accounts for ~33% of attributable risk. The majority of factors are directly influenced by lifestyle.
Yusuf S et al. Lancet 2004;364:937–952
Global modifiable risk factors — NEJM 2022
Addressing five key modifiable risk factors at age 50 — high blood pressure, high cholesterol, obesity, diabetes, smoking — could add more than ten years of cardiovascular disease-free life.
Di Cesare M et al. NEJM 2022
Alcohol — JAMA Network Open 2022
Mendelian randomisation study, 371,463 UK Biobank participants. Genetically predicted alcohol intake associated with increased risk of hypertension and coronary artery disease. Apparent protective effect of moderate drinking largely explained by confounding.
Biddinger KJ et al. JAMA Network Open 2022
Sleep — JAHA meta-analysis 2019
74 studies, 3+ million participants. J-shaped relationship: both short sleep (<6–7 hrs) and long sleep (>8–9 hrs) independently associated with increased cardiovascular mortality. Lowest risk at 7–8 hours per night.
Yin J et al. JAHA 2019
🥗
Try the Mediterranean pattern
More vegetables, fruit, fish, olive oil, legumes. Less processed meat, ultra-processed food. No rigid prescription — a direction.
🚶
Move more — any amount counts
Half the recommended amount still reduces cardiovascular risk by 17%. The biggest gains come from moving from nothing to something.
🚭
If you smoke — stop
The largest single lifestyle intervention available. NHS Stop Smoking Services are significantly more effective than willpower alone.
