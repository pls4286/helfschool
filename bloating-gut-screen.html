How Bloating Actually Happens — The Four Key Mechanisms

🫧 Excess gas production

Gut bacteria ferment undigested FODMAPs in the colon — producing hydrogen, CO₂ and methane gas. More fermentation = more gas = more distension.

🔄 Impaired gas transit

Gas that doesn't move through efficiently builds pressure. Constipation and slow gut motility are major contributors — addressing them often improves bloating significantly.

🧠 Visceral hypersensitivity

Oversensitive gut nerves mean normal amounts of gas feel painful and distressing. Gas production may be entirely normal — the nervous system is amplifying the signal. Central to IBS.

🦠 SIBO — Small Intestinal Bacterial Overgrowth

Bacteria that should be in the colon end up in the small intestine, fermenting food much earlier. Characteristic feature: bloating starts within minutes of eating.
