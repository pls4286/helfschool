Segment 1 — What is the Mediterranean diet?
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read
Slide 2: The plate · food proportion rows animating in · stat card (~30% CV reduction)

"The Mediterranean diet isn't a rulebook. It's a pattern of eating that developed across the coastal regions of southern Europe — and it's now one of the most thoroughly studied dietary patterns in medicine."

"The centrepiece is vegetables and fruit — roughly a third of your plate. Whole grains alongside. Oily fish at least twice a week. Legumes — lentils and chickpeas — regularly in place of meat. And throughout everything, extra-virgin olive oil as the main cooking and dressing fat, replacing butter." PAUSE

"That olive oil swap matters more than most people realise. It shifts your fat intake from saturated to monounsaturated — and that shift is one of the most important changes in the whole pattern."

Segment 2 — Three tiers
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read
Slide 3: Three tiers · daily / several times a week / occasional — rows animating in

"Here's the practical framework. Three tiers."

"Daily, at every meal: vegetables, fruit, whole grains, legumes, nuts, olive oil. This is the bulk. The foundation. The majority of what you eat."

"Several times a week: oily fish — salmon, mackerel, sardines — at least twice. Eggs, poultry, moderate dairy on the other days." PAUSE

"Occasionally, not daily: red meat, processed meat, added sugar, refined grains, ultra-processed food. Not forbidden — just rare."

"The key message is this: abundance, not restriction. No food group is cut out entirely. Studies show that even partial adherence — moving in the direction without following every rule — brings meaningful benefit."

Segment 3 — The PREDIMED evidence
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read
Slide 4: PREDIMED trial · ~30% stat · bar chart animating · trial detail cards

"So what does the evidence actually show? The landmark study is PREDIMED — one of the largest dietary intervention trials ever conducted."

"Seven thousand four hundred and forty-seven people at high cardiovascular risk, randomised to Mediterranean diet with olive oil, Mediterranean diet with nuts, or standard low-fat dietary advice." PAUSE

"The trial was stopped after four point eight years — because the benefit was already too clear to continue ethically."

"The Mediterranean diet group had approximately thirty percent fewer major cardiovascular events — heart attacks, strokes, and cardiovascular deaths — compared to the low-fat control. Published in the New England Journal of Medicine. The strongest dietary evidence in preventive medicine."

Segment 4 — Beyond the heart
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read
Slide 5: Three benefit cards · heart / diabetes / brain · animating in · ~30%, 23%, up to 30%

"What makes the Mediterranean diet unusual is that the evidence extends well beyond cardiovascular disease."

"Type 2 diabetes: a meta-analysis of ten studies and over a hundred and thirty-six thousand participants found twenty-three percent lower risk of developing type 2 diabetes with higher adherence to the Mediterranean diet." PAUSE

"Brain health: a 2025 meta-analysis published in GeroScience found up to thirty percent lower risk of Alzheimer's disease, and around eleven percent lower risk of dementia overall."

"Blood pressure and cholesterol also improve naturally — the diet's high potassium content from vegetables counters sodium, and replacing saturated fat with olive oil and nuts improves LDL cholesterol." PAUSE
