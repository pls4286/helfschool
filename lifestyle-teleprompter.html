▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read · SLIDE 2 — Seven factor cards
Cardiovascular disease develops over decades. The good news — and it is genuinely good news — is that the factors driving that process are largely modifiable. The choices made every day have a measurable effect on long-term risk.
— pause —
This isn't about what's theoretically plausible. It's about what has been tested in large studies, across real populations, and found to make a meaningful difference.
— pause —
The seven factors covered today — diet, physical activity, smoking, body weight, alcohol, stress, and sleep — are not equally powerful, and they don't work in isolation. But together, they represent the most evidence-based levers available.
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read · SLIDE 3 — INTERHEART stat card
The INTERHEART study — over 30,000 people across 52 countries — found that nine modifiable risk factors account for more than 90% of the risk of a first heart attack worldwide. The majority of those factors are directly influenced by lifestyle.
— pause —
A New England Journal of Medicine analysis found that addressing five key modifiable risk factors at age 50 is associated with more than ten years of additional cardiovascular disease-free life. These are not small numbers.
— pause —
And these changes work through multiple pathways at once. Improving diet lowers blood pressure and cholesterol. Exercise reduces insulin resistance. Stopping smoking begins to reduce risk within months. The effects are additive — and in some combinations, more than additive.
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read · SLIDES 4–5 — Mediterranean diet + smoking evidence
On diet — the PREDIMED trial found a Mediterranean diet reduced major cardiovascular events by approximately 30%. That's a randomised controlled trial. Nearly 7,500 people. The strongest dietary evidence available.
— pause —
On physical activity — even modest increases make a meaningful difference. Even half the recommended 150 minutes per week is associated with about a 17% reduction in cardiovascular risk. The biggest gains come from moving from nothing to something.
— pause —
Smoking — the single most powerful modifiable cardiovascular risk factor. The benefits of stopping begin within months. After one year, excess risk is halved. After 15 years, risk approaches that of a lifelong non-smoker.
— pause —
Stress and sleep — chronic psychological stress is an independent predictor of heart attack, contributing around a third of attributable risk in INTERHEART. And both too little and too much sleep independently raise cardiovascular mortality. These are not soft factors.
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read · SLIDES 5–7 — Research cards
PREDIMED — published in the New England Journal of Medicine, 2018 — is the strongest dietary evidence available. Mediterranean diet supplemented with olive oil or nuts: 30% reduction in major cardiovascular events compared to a control diet, over nearly five years.
— pause —
On ultra-processed foods — a 2024 BMJ umbrella review found consistent associations between higher consumption and increased cardiovascular risk, independent of overall diet quality. The associations were strongest for processed meat and sugar-sweetened beverages.
— pause —
On alcohol — the apparent protective effect of moderate drinking has been largely revised away by Mendelian randomisation studies that correct for confounding. For cardiovascular risk, the evidence consistently points to less being better.
— pause —
The message across all the evidence is consistent: direction matters more than perfection. Small, sustained changes in the right direction produce measurable reductions in cardiovascular risk.
