Segment 2 — Why does it matter?
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read
Screen 3: INTERHEART stat card · 90% of heart attack risk from modifiable factors

"The INTERHEART study — over 30,000 people across 52 countries — found that nine modifiable risk factors account for more than 90% of the risk of a first heart attack worldwide. The majority of those factors are directly influenced by lifestyle." PAUSE

"A New England Journal of Medicine analysis found that addressing five key modifiable risk factors at age 50 could add more than ten years of cardiovascular disease-free life. That's an extraordinary number for things that don't require a prescription."

"And these changes work through multiple pathways at once. Improving diet lowers blood pressure and cholesterol. Exercise reduces insulin resistance. Stopping smoking begins to reduce risk within months. The effects are additive — and in some combinations, more than additive." PAUSE

Segment 3 — The evidence by factor
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read
Screen 4: Mediterranean diet card + smoking timeline · PREDIMED 30% · smoking risk reduction

"Let me take you through each factor briefly. Diet — the PREDIMED trial found a Mediterranean diet reduced major cardiovascular events by approximately 30%. That's a randomised controlled trial. Nearly 7,500 people. Robust evidence."

"Physical activity — even modest increases in activity make a meaningful difference. 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week — about 20 minutes a day — is the NICE recommendation. The evidence for even small increases is compelling." PAUSE

"Smoking — the single most powerful modifiable cardiovascular risk factor. The benefits of stopping begin within months. After one year, excess risk is halved. After 15 years, risk approaches that of a non-smoker. Every year matters."

"Stress and sleep — chronic psychological stress dysregulates the autonomic nervous system and is an independent predictor of heart attack. And both too little and too much sleep are independently associated with increased cardiovascular mortality. These are not soft factors." PAUSE

Segment 4 — What the research shows
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read
Screen 5: Research cards · PREDIMED · INTERHEART · NEJM 2022 · BMJ 2024

"PREDIMED — published in the New England Journal of Medicine, 2018 — is the strongest dietary evidence we have. Mediterranean diet supplemented with olive oil or nuts: 30% reduction in major cardiovascular events compared to a control diet, over nearly five years."

"On ultra-processed foods — a 2024 BMJ umbrella review found consistent associations between higher consumption and increased cardiovascular risk, independent of overall diet quality. The associations were strongest for processed meat and sugar-sweetened beverages." PAUSE

"On alcohol — the apparent protective effect of moderate drinking has been largely revised away by more recent Mendelian randomisation studies that correct for confounding. The current NHS and NICE guidance is clear: no level of alcohol consumption is without risk."

"The message across all the evidence is consistent: direction matters more than perfection. Small, sustained changes in the right direction add up — and the evidence says they add up to something clinically meaningful." PAUSE
