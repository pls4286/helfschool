"The INTERHEART study — 29,000 people across 52 countries — found that nine modifiable risk factors account for more than 90% of the risk of a first heart attack worldwide. The majority are directly influenced by lifestyle."

"A New England Journal of Medicine analysis found that addressing five key modifiable risk factors at age 50 — blood pressure, cholesterol, obesity, diabetes, and smoking — could add more than ten years of cardiovascular disease-free life. These are not small numbers." PAUSE

"And the effects stack. Each factor you address adds further protection — and some combinations are more than additive."

Segment 2 — What drives risk up
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read
Slide 3: What drives risk up · six red cards animating in · ultra-processed diet / sedentary / smoking / weight / alcohol / short-long sleep

"So what actually drives cardiovascular risk up? Ultra-processed diets and high sodium raise blood pressure and promote inflammation. Prolonged sitting is now recognised as an independent cardiovascular risk factor — separate from whether you exercise at all." PAUSE

"Current smokers carry two to four times the cardiovascular risk of non-smokers. The Mendelian randomisation evidence on alcohol removes the apparent protective effect — genetically higher alcohol intake is associated with higher blood pressure and coronary disease. And both too little and too much sleep — under six or over nine hours — independently raise cardiovascular mortality."

"On the other side. A Mediterranean eating pattern is associated with around a 30% reduction in major cardiovascular events — from a randomised controlled trial. Segment 4 — Research: modifiable risk
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read
Slide 5: Research — modifiable risk · INTERHEART ~90% / Khera et al −50% CHD events with favourable lifestyle even at high genetic risk

"The INTERHEART study — Yusuf and colleagues, The Lancet, 2004 — found that psychosocial stress alone accounted for approximately 33% of attributable heart attack risk globally. That makes it one of the largest single contributors in the whole dataset." PAUSE

"A 2016 New England Journal of Medicine analysis by Khera and colleagues found something equally important: even in people with the highest genetic cardiovascular risk, a favourable lifestyle — not smoking, healthy weight, regular activity, healthy diet — was associated with roughly 50% lower coronary event rates. Genetics is not cardiovascular destiny."

Segment 5 — Research: diet & activity
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read
Slide 6: Research — diet & activity · PREDIMED −30% CV events · Garcia BJSM 2023 −17% CV risk at 75 min/wk

"PREDIMED — published in the NEJM in 2018. Nearly 7,500 people at high cardiovascular risk, randomised to a Mediterranean diet supplemented with extra-virgin olive oil or nuts. Around 30% reduction in major cardiovascular events compared to a control diet over nearly five years. The strongest dietary evidence we have." PAUSE

"On physical activity — the Garcia meta-analysis in the British Journal of Sports Medicine, 2023. Ninety-four cohorts, over 30 million participants. The most important finding: the greatest marginal gains came from moving from complete inactivity to any regular movement at all. Half the recommended amount — 75 minutes a week — produced a 17% risk reduction."

Segment 6 — Research: smoking & sleep
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read
Slide 7: Research — smoking & sleep · Cochrane −36% CV events after stopping · Yin JAHA 2019 J-curve sleep & CV mortality

"A Cochrane review of smoking cessation and cardiovascular outcomes found that stopping smoking after a cardiovascular event reduced further events by around 36% — larger than almost any single medication. Excess risk approximately halves within a year. Over fifteen years, risk approaches that of a lifelong non-smoker." PAUSE

"On sleep — Yin and colleagues, Journal of the American Heart Association, 2019. 74 studies, over 3 million participants. A J-shaped relationship: both under 6 to 7 hours and over 8 to 9 hours per night were independently associated with higher cardiovascular mortality. Lowest risk sits at 7 to 8 hours."

"These factors work through multiple pathways at once. The Mediterranean pattern lowers LDL and reduces inflammation. Exercise improves vascular function and insulin sensitivity. Stopping smoking begins restoring the endothelium — the inner lining of the blood vessels — within weeks." PAUSE

"Protecting sleep stabilises overnight blood pressure and calms the body's stress axis. Changes in one factor tend to support changes in others — better sleep reduces stress, reduced stress improves dietary choices, regular movement improves sleep."
