Segment 1 — What is a UK unit?
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read
Slide 2: UK units grid · six drink cards animating in · 1.5 / 3.0 / 9.0 / 2.3 / 3.0 / 1.0 units · "14 units/week" guideline strip

"First — the UK unit. One unit is 10 millilitres of pure alcohol." PAUSE

"A small glass of wine — 1.5 units. A large glass — 3. A bottle of wine — 9. A pint of lager — about 2.3. A single spirit — 1 unit."

"The UK Chief Medical Officers' guideline, updated in 2016 — no more than 14 units a week, men and women. Spread over three or more days, with drink-free days in between. 14 units is about six pints of lager, or a bottle and a half of wine — for the whole week." PAUSE

Segment 2 — Five systems alcohol affects
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read
Slide 3: Five systems · five horizontal rows · 🧬 Cancer · ❤️ Heart · 🩸 Liver · 🧠 Brain · 💭 Mood & sleep · red stats on right

"Alcohol reaches every organ. Five systems matter most." PAUSE

"Cancer. Alcohol causes seven cancers — mouth, throat, oesophagus, liver, bowel, and female breast. Group 1 carcinogen — same category as tobacco. In the UK, about 11,900 cancer cases a year are attributable to alcohol."

"Heart and circulation. Raises blood pressure. Triggers atrial fibrillation. Genetics show risk rises with every unit — no protective dose."

"Liver. Three stages — fatty liver, inflammation, cirrhosis. Three-quarters of UK alcohol-specific deaths are from liver disease."

"Brain. MRI studies show shrinkage from one to two units a day. Long-term heavy drinking raises dementia risk."

"And mood and sleep. It fragments sleep, flattens mood, weakens antidepressants. Feels like it helps. Data says it doesn't." PAUSE

Segment 3 — What your GP might do
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read
Slide 4: What your doctor does · six cards · 📋 AUDIT-C · 🩸 bloods · 🔬 FibroScan · 💬 brief intervention · ⚕️ NICE CG115 · 🤝 community

"A GP's first move is the AUDIT-C — three quick questions. Picks up drinking already in the risk zone."

"Blood tests — liver function, GGT, red-cell size — flag chronic heavy use. If the liver looks stressed, a FibroScan measures scarring without a biopsy."

"For drinking above 14 units but not dependent, the NHS answer is a brief intervention — structured conversation about risk, motivation, goals. Cochrane-backed."

"Where there's dependence, NICE CG115 describes stepped care — CBT, acamprosate and naltrexone, and supervised withdrawal. Abrupt withdrawal in heavy drinkers can be dangerous — that's why it's medically supervised." PAUSE

Segment 4 — Research: the Global Burden of Disease
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read
Slide 5: Research A · two evidence cards · GBD 2016 · 2.8M deaths · GBD 2020 · 1.34B drinkers · age-specific curve

"Now the research. This is where the story has changed." PAUSE

"The Global Burden of Disease study, in The Lancet 2018, pooled data from 195 countries. 2.8 million alcohol-attributable deaths globally. Seventh-leading risk factor worldwide. Headline finding: the level that minimises overall health risk is zero."

"The 2022 follow-up refined it by age. For adults under 40, the lowest-risk intake is near zero. For over-40s, a small amount may be net-neutral — but only at intakes well below 14 units a week." PAUSE

Segment 5 — The J-curve debunked
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read
Slide 6: Research B · two evidence cards · Biddinger UK Biobank 1.3× HTN · Zhao 107 studies 4.8M — no mortality benefit

"You've heard that a glass a day is good for you. That idea has largely unravelled." PAUSE

"Biddinger, JAMA Network Open 2022 — UK Biobank data, 371,000 people, using genetic markers as a natural experiment. Each standard-deviation rise in alcohol gave 1.3× the risk of high blood pressure, 1.4× coronary artery disease. Risk rises monotonically. No protective dose."

"Zhao, JAMA Network Open 2023 — pooled 107 studies, 4.8 million people. Corrected for the old bias of classing former heavy drinkers as non-drinkers. Low-to-moderate drinking showed no mortality benefit. The J-curve was an artefact." PAUSE

Segment 6 — Research: brain and dementia
▶ ADVANCE NOW — then read
Slide 7: Research C · two evidence cards · Daviet brain MRI · ~2 yrs ageing at 1→2 units/day · Sabia Whitehall II · +17% dementia per 7-unit rise

"Two more — the brain, and dementia." PAUSE

"Daviet, Nature Communications 2022 — MRI scans of 36,000 UK Biobank adults. Grey and white matter loss detectable from one to two units a day. At age 50, going from one to two units a day equated to brain changes of ageing by about two years."

"Sabia, BMJ 2018 — 9,000 UK civil servants, followed for 23 years. Drinkers above 14 units showed a stepwise dementia rise — about 17% higher risk per 7 units above guideline. The Lancet Commission 2024 confirms alcohol as one of 14 modifiable dementia risk factors." PAUSE

Segment 7 — What to take away
▶ ADVANCE TWICE — past mechanisms (silent), land on CTA
Slide 8 (let mechanisms breathe, no narration) → Slide 9: CTA slide · 🌿 · "Most benefit comes from drinking less than you do now" · chips: 3+ drink-free days · smaller measures · lower ABV · count units

"So what do you take from all this? Less is better. Drink-free days matter." PAUSE

"Stopping completely carries the lowest overall health risk — that option is always there. For those who choose to keep drinking, the evidence is just as clear: any reduction produces measurable benefit. Blood pressure falls within weeks. Sleep improves within days. Fatty liver reverses within months."

"Four practical moves for cutting down. Three or more drink-free days. Smaller measures — 125ml glass instead of 250. Lower-ABV options — 4% beer instead of 5.5%. And count your units — volume × ABV ÷ 1000. Most people underestimate by half." PAUSE
