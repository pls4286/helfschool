The four types of exercise — all four matter

🏃
Type 1 · Aerobic
Heart & lungs
Anything that raises your heart rate for a sustained period. Best-evidenced for heart disease, mental health, and longevity.
Brisk walking · swimming · cycling · running · dancing
💪
Type 2 · Strength
Muscle & bone
Working muscles against resistance. Preserves muscle (declines from ~30) and independently lowers mortality risk.
Weights · resistance bands · press-ups · squats · heavy gardening
🧘
Type 3 · Flexibility
Joints & mobility
Keeps joints moving through their full range. Supports everyday function — reaching, bending, turning.
Yoga · Pilates · tai chi · stretching
⚖️
Type 4 · Balance
Stability & falls
Critical from midlife onwards. Reduces falls — the leading cause of injury in older adults.
Tai chi · single-leg work · dance · fall-prevention classes

What your doctor can help with

❤️
Cardiovascular risk review
Activity advice alongside any medication for raised BP, cholesterol, or elevated QRISK. Guidelines-based targets sit at the heart of the conversation.
🏥
Cardiac rehabilitation
NHS programme after heart attack, bypass, or stent. Combines supervised exercise + education + support. Consistently improves outcomes.
📋
NHS Exercise Referral schemes
Typically 12 weeks of subsidised or free leisure-centre access with an instructor — for people with specific conditions. Local names vary. Ask your GP.
🦴
Physiotherapy
For persistent pain, reduced function, injury, or osteoporosis. Often self-referral. Graded return to activity is the goal.
💊
"Pill vs exercise" — not a choice
Statins (e.g. atorvastatin / Lipitor) lower cholesterol. Exercise lowers cholesterol, BP, inflammation, and glucose — through different mechanisms. Exercise makes medication more effective — it doesn't replace it.

"10,000 steps" was a marketing slogan
Yamasa Tokei Keiki launched a pedometer called Manpo-kei — literally "10,000 steps meter". Catchy round number. Never based on evidence.
