The four types of exercise — all four matter
🏃
Type 1 · Aerobic
Heart & lungs
Anything that raises your heart rate for a sustained period. Best-evidenced for heart disease, mental health, and longevity.
Brisk walking · swimming · cycling · running · dancing
💪
Type 2 · Strength
Muscle & bone
Working muscles against resistance. Preserves muscle (declines from ~30) and independently lowers mortality risk.
Weights · bands · press-ups · squats · heavy gardening
🧘
Type 3 · Flexibility
Joints & mobility
Keeps joints moving through their full range. Supports everyday function — reaching, bending, turning.
Yoga · Pilates · tai chi · stretching
⚖️
Type 4 · Balance
Stability & falls
Critical from midlife onwards. Reduces falls — a leading cause of injury in older adults.
Tai chi · single-leg work · dance · fall-prevention classes

What your doctor can help with — 1 of 2
❤️
Cardiovascular risk review
Activity advice alongside any medication for raised blood pressure, cholesterol, or elevated QRISK. Guidelines-based targets anchor the conversation.

Cardiac rehabilitation
NHS programme after heart attack, bypass, or stent. Supervised exercise + education + support — consistently improves outcomes.
NHS

What your doctor can help with — 2 of 2

Physiotherapy
For persistent pain, reduced function, injury, or osteoporosis. Often self-referral in the NHS. Graded return to activity is the goal.

"Pill vs exercise" — not a choice
Cholesterol-lowering medication lowers cholesterol. Exercise lowers cholesterol, BP, inflammation and glucose — through different mechanisms. Exercise makes medication more effective — it doesn't replace it.
Both, not either
