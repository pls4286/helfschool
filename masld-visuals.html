Fatty Liver Disease —
MASLD, the silent epidemic
Why 1 in 5 UK adults are affected, why most don't know, and what the evidence says about reversing it.
Dr Paul · Retired NHS GP · helf.school
The MASLD spectrum — from fat to serious disease
Steatosis
Fat in liver cells · no inflammation · reversible with lifestyle change
→
MASH
Fat + inflammation + cell damage · previously called NASH
→
Fibrosis
Scar tissue forming · still reversible at early stages
→
Cirrhosis
Advanced scarring · largely irreversible · liver failure risk rises
→
HCC
Liver cancer (hepatocellular carcinoma) · can occur without cirrhosis
1 in 5
UK adults estimated to have MASLD — around 10 million people, and roughly half are currently undiagnosed. The liver has no pain receptors, so most stages are silent until advanced ⚑.
What drives MASLD — the main risk factors
⚖️
Central obesity — abdominal fat
Visceral fat around the liver drives steatosis more powerfully than BMI (body mass index) alone.
Top driver
🩸
Type 2 diabetes & insulin resistance
Most people with T2D have MASLD. Insulin resistance is the central metabolic driver of liver fat accumulation.
Metabolic
📈
High triglycerides · low HDL
Classic metabolic dyslipidaemia — the liver overproduces triglycerides under insulin resistance.
Lipids
🍟
Ultra-processed food & added sugar
Fructose-heavy diets are metabolised by the liver and drive fat deposition and inflammation.
Diet
🛋️
Physical inactivity
Exercise reduces liver fat independently of weight loss. Sedentary patterns accelerate disease progression.

What your doctor might do
🧮
FIB-4 score — first-line in primary care
Calculated from age, AST, ALT, platelets — bloods already available in UK primary care. Identifies who needs onward assessment. Recommended by EASL 2024.
1st-line
📡
Imaging — ultrasound & FibroScan
Liver ultrasound confirms steatosis. FibroScan (transient elastography) estimates liver stiffness — non-invasive assessment of fibrosis severity.
Imaging
🩺
Specialist referral — advanced fibrosis
Hepatology referral when FIB-4 elevated or imaging suggests ≥ F2 fibrosis. Ongoing specialist surveillance for cirrhosis and HCC (hepatocellular carcinoma) risk.
Hepatology
💊
Pharmacotherapy — selected cases
Resmetirom (Rezdiffra) for non-cirrhotic MASH with ≥ F2 fibrosis where approved. GLP-1 agonists: semaglutide (Ozempic/Wegovy), tirzepatide (Mounjaro) — particularly where T2D or significant obesity.
Targeted Rx
🥗
Lifestyle support — the foundation
Dietitian-led Mediterranean pattern, structured exercise referral, weight-management support. The cornerstone of treatment at every stage.

🥗 Diet — Mediterranean pattern

12-week RCT: Mediterranean diet reduced liver fat by ~39% and improved insulin sensitivity vs standard low-fat diet. High vegetables, legumes, fish, olive oil. Low ultra-processed food, added sugar, refined carbs.

☕ Coffee — genuinely evidence-based

2–3 cups per day associated with slower disease progression. Anti-inflammatory and antifibrotic effects. Explicitly recommended in EASL 2024 guidelines — one of the more welcome evidence-based interventions.

What the evidence actually shows
UK standard
EASL/EASD/EASO joint guideline
EASL/EASD/EASO Clinical Practice Guidelines
Journal of Hepatology · 2024 · 81(3):492–542
FIB-4 as first-line fibrosis assessment in primary care. Lifestyle modification as primary treatment. GLP-1 receptor agonists for eligible patients. Resmetirom (Rezdiffra) for non-cirrhotic MASH with ≥ F2 fibrosis where approved ⚑.
≥10%
weight loss reverses fibrosis
Romero-Gómez et al. — weight loss thresholds
Journal of Hepatology · 2017 · 67(4):829–846
≥5% weight loss reduces steatosis. ≥7% improves necroinflammation. ≥10% stabilises or reverses fibrosis — including at F2–F3 stage. Benefit maintained only while weight loss is sustained. Basis for all international guidance ⚑.
~39%
reduction in liver fat · Mediterranean diet
Mediterranean diet RCT — 12-week intervention
Reviewed: Karimi et al. Int J Mol Sci · 2025 · 26(19):9625
12-week RCT: Mediterranean diet reduced hepatic steatosis by ~39% and improved insulin sensitivity vs standard low-fat diet. Consistently outperforms other dietary patterns across MASLD trials ⚑.
~10M
UK adults with MASLD · ~half undiagnosed
British Liver Trust — UK prevalence alert
British Liver Trust · June 2025 · GlobalData analysis
~10M UK adults have MASLD; only ~5.2M diagnosed. ~4.8M undiagnosed. Forecast to rise to 10.4M by 2032. Urgent call for MASLD to receive same clinical attention as other major chronic conditions ⚑.
