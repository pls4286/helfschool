Fatty Liver Disease —
MASLD, the silent epidemic
Why 1 in 5 UK adults are affected, why most don't know, and what the evidence shows about reversing it.
Dr Paul · Retired NHS GP · helf.school
The MASLD spectrum — from fat to serious disease
Steatosis
Fat in liver cells · no inflammation · reversible with lifestyle
→
MASH
Fat + inflammation + cell damage · previously called NASH
→
Fibrosis
Scar tissue forming · still reversible at early stages
→
Cirrhosis
Advanced scarring · largely irreversible · liver failure risk rises
→
HCC
Liver cancer (hepatocellular carcinoma) · can occur without cirrhosis
1 in 5
UK adults estimated to have MASLD — around 10 million people. The liver has no pain receptors, so most stages are completely silent. Many are undiagnosed ⚑.
What drives MASLD — main risk factors
1 of 2
⚖️
Central obesity — abdominal fat
Visceral fat around the liver drives steatosis more powerfully than BMI alone. Waist circumference is the key clinical measure.
Top driver
🩸
Type 2 diabetes & insulin resistance
Insulin resistance is the central metabolic driver of liver fat accumulation. Most people with type 2 diabetes have MASLD.
Metabolic
📈
High triglycerides & low HDL
Classic metabolic dyslipidaemia — the liver overproduces triglycerides under insulin resistance. Raises cardiovascular risk alongside liver disease.

What drives MASLD — diet & activity
2 of 2
🍟
Ultra-processed food & added sugar
Fructose-heavy diets — refined carbohydrates and added sugars — drive liver fat deposition and promote active inflammation.
Diet
🛋️
Physical inactivity
Exercise reduces liver fat independently of weight loss. Physical inactivity accelerates disease progression at every stage of MASLD.

What your doctor might discuss
1 of 2 — assessment
🧮
FIB-4 score — first-line assessment
Calculated from age, AST (a liver enzyme), ALT (a liver enzyme), and platelet count — all available from routine NHS blood tests. Identifies who needs onward investigation. EASL 2024 first-line recommendation.
1st-line
📡
Imaging — ultrasound & FibroScan
Liver ultrasound confirms steatosis (fat in the liver). FibroScan — transient elastography — measures liver stiffness as a non-invasive marker of fibrosis severity.
Imaging
🩺
Specialist referral — significant fibrosis
Hepatology referral when FIB-4 is elevated or imaging suggests significant fibrosis. Ongoing surveillance for cirrhosis and liver cancer risk where indicated.
Hepatology
What your doctor might discuss
2 of 2 — treatment
💊
Pharmacotherapy — selected cases
A MASH-specific approved drug class is now available for non-cirrhotic MASH with significant fibrosis. GLP-1 receptor agonists and dual GLP-1/GIP agonists are considered where obesity or type 2 diabetes is present.
Targeted Rx
🥗
Lifestyle support — the foundation
Mediterranean-pattern diet, structured exercise, and weight management support are the cornerstone of treatment at every stage of MASLD.

🥗 Diet — Mediterranean pattern
High vegetables, legumes, fish, and olive oil. Low ultra-processed food, added sugar, and refined carbohydrates. The evidence consistently shows significant reduction in liver fat and improved insulin sensitivity — ranked as the best dietary pattern for MASLD.
☕ Coffee — genuinely evidence-based
2–3 cups per day associated with slower liver disease progression. Anti-inflammatory and antifibrotic effects. Explicitly included in EASL 2024 international guidelines — one of the more welcome evidence-based findings in liver medicine.

Weight loss thresholds — MASH evidence summary
Journal of Hepatology · 2017 · 67(4):829–846 ⚑
≥5% weight loss reduces liver fat. ≥7% improves active inflammation. ≥10% stabilises or reverses fibrosis including F2–F3. Benefit persists while weight loss is maintained.
