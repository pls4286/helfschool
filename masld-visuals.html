The MASLD spectrum — from fatty liver to serious disease
Steatosis
Fat in liver cells · no inflammation · often reversible with lifestyle change
→
MASH
Fat + inflammation + cell damage · previously called NASH · can progress to fibrosis
→
Fibrosis
Scar tissue forming · reversible at early stages with significant intervention
→
Cirrhosis
Advanced scarring · largely irreversible · liver failure risk rises significantly
→
HCC
Liver cancer · MASLD is a leading cause · can occur without cirrhosis

British Liver Trust / GlobalData 2025 — the diagnosis gap

~10 million people in the UK have MASLD. Only ~5.2 million are diagnosed — roughly half go unidentified. A condition that, if identified and acted on early, can be reversed — not just managed. Early identification changes outcomes.

Diet — Mediterranean pattern

12-week RCT: Mediterranean diet reduced liver fat by 39% and improved insulin sensitivity. High vegetables, legumes, fish, olive oil. Low ultra-processed food, added sugar, refined carbs.

Coffee — genuinely evidence-based

2–3 cups per day associated with slower disease progression. Anti-inflammatory and antifibrotic effects. Explicitly recommended in EASL 2024 guidelines. One of the more unusual — and welcome — evidence-based interventions.

What the research shows
EASL/EASD/EASO Clinical Practice Guidelines — J Hepatol 2024
The current international standard. FIB-4 as first-line fibrosis assessment in primary care. Lifestyle modification as primary treatment. GLP-1 receptor agonists for eligible patients. Resmetirom (Rezdiffra) for non-cirrhotic MASH with significant fibrosis (≥F2) where approved. No pharmacotherapy recommended at cirrhotic stage.
Key guideline · EASL/EASD/EASO Joint Guidelines · J Hepatol 2024;81(3):492–542
Weight loss thresholds — Romero-Gómez et al., J Hepatol 2017
≥5% weight loss reduces steatosis. ≥7% improves necroinflammation. ≥10% stabilises or reverses fibrosis — including at F2–F3 stage. Benefit maintained only while weight loss is sustained. Basis for all international guidance.
Romero-Gómez M et al. · J Hepatol 2017;67(4):829–846 · PMID 28545937
Mediterranean diet — 39% reduction in liver fat · RCT evidence
12-week RCT, 259 participants. Mediterranean diet reduced hepatic steatosis by 39% and improved insulin sensitivity vs standard low-fat diet. Consistently outperforms other dietary patterns across MASLD trials.
Reviewed in: Karimi F et al. Int J Mol Sci 2025;26(19):9625

Diagnosis gap — the public health opportunity
British Liver Trust — June 2025
Urgent action needed as new research reveals critical gaps in fatty liver disease diagnosis and care. MASLD is estimated to affect up to 1 in 5 people in the UK. Most remain undiagnosed. The British Liver Trust called for MASLD to receive the same clinical attention as other major chronic conditions. Rates are forecast to rise from 9.9 million cases in 2024 to 10.4 million by 2032.
British Liver Trust · britishlivertrust.org.uk · June 2025 · based on GlobalData epidemiological analysis
🩺
Know the picture
Obesity, type 2 diabetes, raised waist circumference, elevated liver enzymes on routine blood tests — the NICE-recommended next step in this picture is a FIB-4 score, calculated from bloods already available in UK primary care.
🥗
Lifestyle is the treatment
Mediterranean diet. Weight loss. Exercise. Reduce ultra-processed food and added sugar. Even 5% weight loss reduces liver fat. 10% can reverse fibrosis.
☕
Enjoy your coffee
2–3 cups per day associated with slower liver disease progression. Anti-inflammatory and antifibrotic effects. Explicitly recommended by EASL 2024 guidelines.
