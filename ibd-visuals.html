Crohn's Disease vs Ulcerative Colitis

Crohn's Disease

LocationAnywhere — mouth to anus
PatternSkip lesions (patchy)
DepthTransmural — full thickness
HallmarkPain, diarrhoea, weight loss
Perianal diseaseCommon
SmokingWorsens disease
SurgeryNot curative
VS

Ulcerative Colitis

LocationColon and rectum only
PatternContinuous from rectum
DepthMucosal — inner lining only
HallmarkBloody diarrhoea, urgency
Perianal diseaseRare
SmokingLowers risk*
SurgeryColectomy is curative

*Smoking is NOT recommended — overall harms far outweigh any gut benefit

🧬
Genetics
Over 200 genetic loci associated with IBD — many involved in immune regulation and gut barrier function
🦠
Gut microbiome
Consistently reduced diversity and altered bacterial composition in IBD patients — dysbiosis
🛡️
Immune system
Abnormal immune response — attacks gut contents in genetically susceptible individuals
🌍
Environment
Rising incidence in newly industrialised countries — diet, antibiotics, hygiene hypothesis, urban living
Treatment — a stepped approach
1
Aminosalicylates (5-ASA) — for UC
Mesalazine — first-line for mild to moderate ulcerative colitis. Reduces mucosal inflammation and maintains remission. Less effective in Crohn's disease.
2
Corticosteroids — for inducing remission
Prednisolone, budesonide — effective for active flares but not for long-term maintenance. Goal is always to taper and stop. Prolonged use causes significant side effects.
3
Immunomodulators — for maintaining remission
Azathioprine, mercaptopurine, methotrexate — suppress immune response to maintain remission. Take months to reach full effect. Require regular blood monitoring.
4
Biologics & small molecules — for moderate–severe disease
Anti-TNF (infliximab, adalimumab) · Vedolizumab · Ustekinumab · JAK inhibitors (tofacitinib, upadacitinib). Have transformed IBD management over 20 years.

Global epidemiology — Ng et al., The Lancet 2017
Systematic review of 43 countries. IBD has become a global disease. UK has among the highest rates worldwide. Incidence accelerating in newly industrialised countries across Asia, South America, and Africa.
Ng SC et al. Lancet 2017;390:2769–2778 · PMID 29050646
UK prevalence — Crohn's & Colitis UK, 2022
Analysis of 38.3 million GP records. Over 500,000 people (1 in 123) living with IBD in the UK — nearly double the previous estimate of 300,000. Prevalence: UC 0.44%, Crohn's 0.31%. Adolescent incidence +3%/year.
Crohn's & Colitis UK / University of Nottingham, 2022
Colorectal cancer risk — Jess et al., Clin Gastroenterol Hepatol 2012
Meta-analysis of population-based cohorts. Ulcerative colitis increases CRC risk 2.4-fold vs general population. Extensive colitis: risk rises to ~4-fold. Evidence base for surveillance colonoscopy programme.
Jess T et al. Clin Gastroenterol Hepatol 2012;10:639–645 · PMID 22289873
NICE guidelines NG129 & NG130 / BSG guidelines, 2019
UK standard for Crohn's and UC management. BSG guidelines: 88,247 publications reviewed, 168 recommendations. Both emphasise IBD MDT and active treatment of psychological comorbidity — anxiety and depression highly prevalent.
NICE NG129 / NG130 · Lamb CA et al. Gut 2019;68(suppl 3):s1–s106 · PMID 31562236
🔍
Get an accurate diagnosis
IBD and IBS are entirely different conditions. Getting the right diagnosis matters — faecal calprotectin is the key first step.
👥
Work with a specialist team
Gastroenterologist, IBD nurse, dietitian, surgeon, psychologist. The IBD MDT exists to help — use it.
📅
Stay engaged with follow-up
Surveillance colonoscopy, medication monitoring, psychological support. IBD requires ongoing specialist engagement.
