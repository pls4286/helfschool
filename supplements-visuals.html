What the research shows
SPORT Trial — JACC 2023 · Cleveland Clinic · 190 participants
Rosuvastatin 5mg reduced LDL by 37.9%. Six supplements (fish oil, cinnamon, garlic, turmeric, plant sterols, red yeast rice) showed no significant LDL reduction vs placebo. Garlic slightly increased LDL.
Laffin LJ et al. J Am Coll Cardiol 2023;81(1):1–12
Plant sterol meta-analysis — Br J Nutr 2014
124 randomised trials. Plant sterols at 2g/day: LDL reduction of 7–12%. Effect real and consistent. But no large trial has shown this translates into fewer cardiovascular events.
Ras RT et al. Br J Nutr 2014
Fish oil & AF risk — BMJ Medicine 2024
UK Biobank, 415,000 participants. Regular fish oil use associated with 13% higher atrial fibrillation risk and 5% higher stroke risk in those without existing cardiovascular disease.
Chen et al. BMJ Medicine 2024
EFSA — January 2025
Red yeast rice: no safe dose of monacolin K in food supplements. EU-wide ban in progress. Same risks as prescription lovastatin without regulatory oversight or monitoring.
European Food Safety Authority, January 2025
🔍
Understand what it does
Does the supplement lower LDL? Triglycerides? Has it been tested in outcome trials? The difference between these questions matters enormously.
⚠️
Be cautious about red yeast rice
Effectively an unregulated statin. EFSA 2025 — no safe dose. Tell your GP if you're taking it.
💬
Don't avoid the statin conversation
If your cardiovascular risk is elevated, that conversation has an evidence base no supplement can match.
