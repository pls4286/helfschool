Types of Cholesterol

HDL

HDL Cholesterol

"Good cholesterol" — carries cholesterol away from arteries to the liver for disposal

LDL

LDL Cholesterol

"Bad cholesterol" — can build up in artery walls, causing narrowing and blockages

Total cholesterol = HDL + LDL + other lipoproteins

Target Levels

Total Cholesterol
< 5.0 mmol/L
LDL Cholesterol
< 3.0 mmol/L
HDL Cholesterol (Men)
> 1.0 mmol/L
HDL Cholesterol (Women)
> 1.2 mmol/L
High Risk Patients
LDL < 1.8 mmol/L

What the Research Shows

Statin Effectiveness

22% reduction

Statins reduce major cardiovascular events by 22% per 1 mmol/L LDL reduction

Cholesterol Treatment Trialists • 2010

Lifetime Exposure

50% lower risk

People with genetically low LDL have 50% lower coronary heart disease risk

NEJM • 2006

Diet vs Genetics

80% genetic

Blood cholesterol levels are primarily determined by genetics, not diet

BMJ • 2013

Primary Prevention

36% stroke reduction

Statins prevent first heart attacks and strokes in high-risk people

Cochrane Review • 2013

Key Takeaways

• Cholesterol levels are mostly genetic — diet has limited impact

• LDL cholesterol is the main target for cardiovascular protection

• Statins are highly effective at reducing cardiovascular events

• Regular testing needed — cholesterol usually has no symptoms
