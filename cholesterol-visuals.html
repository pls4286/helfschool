Cholesterol —
the full picture
What cholesterol is, why LDL matters most, and what the evidence shows about lowering it.
Dr Paul · Retired NHS GP · helf.school
The two main cholesterol types
Protective
HDL
Carries cholesterol away from artery walls to the liver for disposal.
Men > 1.0 · Women > 1.2 mmol/L
Harmful at high levels
LDL
Deposits cholesterol into artery walls — the main driver of atherosclerosis (arterial plaque build-up).
Target < 3.0 mmol/L · High-risk < 2.0
💡
Total cholesterol = HDL + LDL + other lipoproteins · non-HDL cholesterol is the modern clinical treatment target · NICE NG238
Why it matters: where cholesterol causes harm
❤️
Heart — coronary disease
Main driver of angina, heart attack, and sudden cardiac death. Each 1 mmol/L LDL rise significantly increases major vascular event risk.
1 mmol/L
🧠
Brain — ischaemic stroke
Atherosclerosis in neck and brain arteries triggers ischaemic stroke (brain tissue damage from blocked blood supply) and transient ischaemic attacks (TIA).
Stroke
🦵
Legs — peripheral arterial disease
Leg-artery narrowing causes exercise pain (claudication — cramping due to inadequate blood flow); in severe cases, tissue loss and amputation risk.
PAD
🩸
Aorta — aneurysm and dissection
Atherosclerotic weakening of the aorta (the body's main artery) contributes to abdominal aortic aneurysm and risk of aortic dissection.
Aorta
🧬
Inherited — familial hypercholesterolaemia
Affects roughly 1 in 250 UK adults — most undiagnosed. Untreated FH drives early heart attack, often before age 55 in men and 60 in women.
1 in 250
What your doctor might do
🩸
Full lipid panel
Total, HDL, non-HDL, and triglycerides. Fasting no longer required for most adults under NICE NG238.
Test
🧮
QRISK3 cardiovascular risk calculation
Estimates 10-year cardiovascular risk using age, sex, blood pressure, cholesterol, family history, and comorbidities.
Calculate
🔍
Rule out secondary causes
Thyroid, kidney and liver function, diabetes screen, and review of any medications known to raise lipid levels.
Exclude
🌿
Lifestyle measures
Mediterranean or DASH-style eating, regular aerobic activity, weight management, and reducing saturated fat and alcohol are all clinically recognised as improving lipid profiles.
Lifestyle
💊
Statin ± ezetimibe — NICE NG238 pathway
Atorvastatin is first line. Ezetimibe (a non-statin that reduces cholesterol absorption) is added when LDL target is not reached on statin alone.
Treat
🏥
Specialist lipid clinic
For suspected familial hypercholesterolaemia, statin intolerance, or where PCSK9 inhibitors (a newer drug class) may be indicated.
Refer

Scale of raised cholesterol in the UK
NHS Health Survey for England · NHS Digital
Most people with raised cholesterol have no symptoms — the condition is detectable only through a blood test. The NHS Health Check programme offers testing every five years to everyone aged 40–74 in England, but detection rates remain incomplete.

Cholesterol is largely determined by genetics
Twin and family studies · multiple cohorts
Diet plays a meaningful role, but for most people genetics sets the dial. Family history of early heart disease raises the clinical case for earlier cholesterol assessment, even in people who appear healthy. This is especially true for familial hypercholesterolaemia.
Research · LDL lowering works
Key finding
~22%
reduction in major vascular events
per 1 mmol/L fall in LDL · CTT meta-analysis · 170,000 participants · The Lancet 2010
CTT meta-analysis — 26 trials · 170,000 participants
Baigent et al · The Lancet · 2010
Every 1 mmol/L reduction in LDL cholesterol cut major vascular events by approximately 22%, coronary deaths by ~20%, and all-cause mortality by ~10%. The benefit was consistent regardless of starting LDL level, age, or sex — one of the most robust findings in cardiovascular medicine.
Key finding
6.4%
additional reduction in cardiovascular events
from adding ezetimibe to statin therapy · IMPROVE-IT · NEJM 2015 · n=18,144
IMPROVE-IT — ezetimibe added to statin
Cannon et al · NEJM · 2015 · n=18,144
Adding ezetimibe to statin therapy produced a further LDL reduction of approximately 0.4 mmol/L and a 6.4% relative reduction in cardiovascular events over 7 years. This confirmed that further LDL lowering beyond statins alone translates into additional clinical benefit.
Research · lifetime LDL exposure matters
Key finding
~55%
lower coronary heart disease risk
per 1 mmol/L lower lifetime LDL — Mendelian randomisation · n=312,321 · JACC 2012
Mendelian randomisation — lifetime LDL and CHD risk
Ference et al · JACC · 2012 · n=312,321
Using genetic variants as a natural experiment, each 1 mmol/L lower LDL sustained from birth corresponded to approximately 55% lower coronary heart disease risk — roughly three times the effect seen in short-term drug trials. This finding underlines the clinical importance of earlier intervention in younger people with elevated LDL.
Key finding
×20
higher risk of early myocardial infarction in untreated FH
vs general population · Simon Broome Register · NICE CG71
Familial hypercholesterolaemia — untreated risk
NICE CG71 · Simon Broome Register
Untreated familial hypercholesterolaemia carries approximately a 20-fold higher risk of early coronary disease. Early statin treatment brings cardiovascular risk close to population baseline — making cascade screening of first-degree relatives a recognised clinical priority in UK guidelines.
How cholesterol drives heart disease
🔓
LDL wall entry
Small LDL particles penetrate the endothelium (inner lining of the artery) into the vessel wall itself.
Wall entry
🧪
LDL oxidation
Inside the arterial wall, LDL is oxidised — converting it into a potent inflammatory trigger.
Ox-LDL
🧫
Foam cell formation
Macrophages (immune cells) engulf oxidised LDL and become bloated "foam cells" — unable to leave the vessel wall.
Foam cells
🛡️
Plaque build-up
Layers of foam cells, lipid debris, and fibrous tissue form an atherosclerotic plaque that progressively narrows the artery.
Plaque
💥
Plaque rupture
The fibrous cap tears, exposing the lipid core to the blood. Rupture is often unpredictable and occurs without warning.
Rupture
🩸
Clot and vessel occlusion
Platelets form a clot on the ruptured plaque, rapidly blocking blood flow — the mechanism behind heart attack and ischaemic stroke.
MI / stroke

High cholesterol is clinically silent — only a blood test can detect it

Each 1 mmol/L LDL reduction cuts major cardiovascular events by ~22%
A consistent finding across 170,000 participants in randomised trials — the evidence base for statin therapy is among the most robust in medicine.
−22%
🧬
Lower LDL from birth is associated with ~55% lower lifetime CHD risk
Genetic evidence confirms LDL is causal — and that the earlier it is reduced, the greater the long-term cardiovascular protection.
−55%
💊
No supplement matches statin therapy for LDL reduction
The SPORT trial found ~38% LDL reduction from low-dose statin vs no significant reduction from fish oil, garlic, cinnamon, turmeric, plant sterols, or red yeast rice.
38%
