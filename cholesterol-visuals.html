Cholesterol
the full picture
What cholesterol is, why LDL matters most, and what the evidence really says about lowering it.
Dr Paul · Retired NHS GP · helf.school
The two main cholesterol types
Protective
HDL
Carries cholesterol away from artery walls to the liver for disposal.
Men > 1.0 · Women > 1.2 mmol/L
Harmful at high levels
LDL
Deposits cholesterol into artery walls — the main driver of atherosclerosis.
Target < 3.0 mmol/L · High-risk < 1.8
💡
Total cholesterol = HDL + LDL + other lipoproteins · non-HDL cholesterol is the modern treatment target
Why it matters: where cholesterol causes harm
❤️
Heart — coronary disease
Main driver of angina, heart attack, and sudden cardiac death. Each 1 mmol/L LDL rise raises major vascular events.
1 mmol/L
🧠
Brain — ischaemic stroke
Atherosclerosis in neck and brain arteries triggers ischaemic stroke and transient ischaemic attacks (TIA).
Stroke
🦵
Legs — peripheral arterial disease
Leg-artery narrowing causes exercise pain (claudication); in severe cases, tissue loss and amputation risk.
PAD
🩸
Aorta — aneurysm & dissection
Atherosclerotic weakening of the aorta contributes to abdominal aortic aneurysm and dissection risk.
Aorta
🧬
Inherited — familial hypercholesterolaemia
Affects roughly 1 in 250 UK adults. Untreated, drives early heart attack — often before age 55 in men, 60 in women.
1 in 250
What your doctor might do
🩸
Full lipid panel
Total, HDL, non-HDL, and triglycerides. Fasting no longer required for most adults (NICE NG238).
→ Test
🧮
QRISK3 risk calculation
Estimates 10-year cardiovascular risk using age, sex, BP, cholesterol, family history, and comorbidities.
→ Calculate
🔍
Rule out secondary causes
Thyroid, kidney and liver function, diabetes screen, and a review of any medications that raise lipids.
→ Exclude
🌿
Lifestyle guidance
Mediterranean / DASH-style eating, regular activity, weight management, and limiting alcohol and saturated fats.
→ Advise
💊
Statin ± ezetimibe
NICE NG238 pathway — atorvastatin (Lipitor) is first line. Ezetimibe added when LDL target not reached.
→ Treat
🏥
Specialist lipid clinic
For suspected familial hypercholesterolaemia, statin intolerance, or when PCSK9 inhibitors may be needed.
→ Refer
Research · the UK picture
~50%
UK adults with raised total cholesterol (≥5 mmol/L)
Scale of raised cholesterol in the UK
NHS Health Survey for England · BHF data
Raised cholesterol is one of the most common modifiable cardiovascular risk factors in the UK. Most people have no symptoms — detection needs a blood test.
40–60%
heritability of LDL cholesterol in twin studies
Cholesterol is largely genetic
Twin & family studies · multiple cohorts
Diet plays a role, but genetics sets the dial. Family history of early heart disease raises the case for checking cholesterol earlier, even in people who look healthy.
Research · LDL lowering works
−22%
major vascular events per 1 mmol/L ↓ LDL
CTT meta-analysis — 26 trials
Baigent et al · The Lancet · 2010 · n=170,000
Every 1 mmol/L reduction in LDL cholesterol cut major vascular events by ~22%, coronary deaths by ~20%, and all-cause mortality by ~10%.
−6.4%
CV events · ezetimibe added to statin
IMPROVE-IT trial
Cannon et al · NEJM · 2015 · n=18,144
Adding ezetimibe (Ezetrol) to statin therapy produced a further LDL drop of ~0.4 mmol/L and a 6.4% relative reduction in CV events over 7 years.
Research · lifetime exposure matters
~55%
lower CHD risk per 1 mmol/L lifetime ↓ LDL
Mendelian randomisation — Ference
Ference et al · JACC · 2012 · n=312,321
Using genes as a natural experiment, each 1 mmol/L lower LDL sustained from birth corresponds to about a 55% reduction in coronary heart disease — roughly three-fold the short-term trial effect.
×20
early MI risk · untreated FH vs general pop.
Familial hypercholesterolaemia — NICE CG71
NICE CG71 · Simon Broome Register
Untreated FH carries a ~20-fold higher risk of early coronary disease. Early statin treatment brings risk close to population baseline — strong case for cascade screening.
How cholesterol drives heart disease
🔓
LDL breach
Small LDL particles slip through the artery's inner lining (endothelium) into the wall itself.
→ Wall entry
🧪
LDL oxidation
Inside the wall, LDL is oxidised — which converts it into an inflammatory trigger.
→ Ox-LDL
🧫
Foam cell formation
Macrophages (immune cells) engulf oxidised LDL and become bloated "foam cells" that cannot leave.
→ Foam cells
🛡️
Plaque build-up
Layers of foam cells, lipid debris, and fibrous tissue form an atherosclerotic plaque that narrows the artery.
→ Plaque
💥
Plaque rupture
The fibrous cap tears, exposing the lipid core. Unstable plaques often rupture unpredictably.
→ Rupture
🩸
Clot and occlusion
Platelets rapidly form a clot on the ruptured plaque, blocking the vessel — causing a heart attack or ischaemic stroke.
→ MI / stroke

The earlier, the better.
Lifetime LDL exposure is what drives risk.
A 1 mmol/L drop in LDL cuts major cardiovascular events by around a fifth. The longer the lower number is held, the bigger the protection.
