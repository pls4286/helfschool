Hypertension
— the silent condition
What high blood pressure is, why it matters, and what the evidence says about treatment.
Dr Paul · Retired NHS GP · helf.school
What is blood pressure?
140
Systolic
Pressure when the heart beats
/
90
Diastolic
Pressure between beats
⚠️
140/90 mmHg or above on more than one occasion = clinical diagnosis threshold · NICE NG136
Why it matters: five body systems — 1 of 2
❤️
Heart
Coronary artery disease, heart attack, heart failure. Every 20 mmHg rise in systolic pressure doubles cardiovascular mortality risk.
2×
🧠
Brain
Leading modifiable risk factor for stroke. Also drives vascular dementia through small-vessel injury — brain damage caused by impaired blood supply.
Stroke
🫘
Kidneys
Second-leading cause of kidney failure in the UK after diabetes — often clinically silent until an advanced stage.
2nd
Why it matters: five body systems — 2 of 2
👁️
Eyes
Hypertensive retinopathy — damage to the retinal blood vessels — with haemorrhages and permanent vision loss in advanced cases.
Retina
🩸
Arteries
Accelerates atherosclerosis — arterial plaque build-up — triggers aortic aneurysms, and drives peripheral arterial disease.
PAD
What your doctor might do — 1 of 2
🩺
Clinic BP measurement
Multiple readings over several visits — a single high reading alone does not confirm a diagnosis.
Check
📊
Ambulatory / home monitoring (ABPM)
24-hour ABPM or home readings confirm the diagnosis and avoid white coat hypertension — NICE NG136 recommends this before treatment.
Confirm
🩸
Blood and urine tests
Kidney function, cholesterol, blood glucose, urine albumin — screens for both causes and consequences of raised blood pressure.
Test
What your doctor might do — 2 of 2
💓
ECG (electrocardiogram)
Looks for left ventricular hypertrophy — thickening of the heart muscle — a sign of long-standing pressure strain.
Trace
🌿
Lifestyle measures
Salt reduction, weight management, physical activity, alcohol moderation, and DASH-style eating are all clinically recognised as lowering blood pressure.
Lifestyle
💊
Medication — NICE NG136 pathway
ACE inhibitor / ARB, calcium channel blocker, or thiazide-like diuretic — often combined. Specialist referral for resistant hypertension.
Treat
Research · the UK picture
Key finding
1 in 3
UK adults have high blood pressure
rising to more than half of those aged 65 and over — NHS Digital · Health Survey for England 2022
Scale of hypertension in the UK
NHS Digital · Health Survey for England · ONS
Roughly 14 million people across England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland are affected. Prevalence rises steeply with age — around 60% of those aged 65 and over, and 66–71% of those aged 75 and over.
Key finding
~50%
undiagnosed or not reaching blood pressure targets
of those with hypertension in the UK — symptoms rarely appear until damage is advanced
The silent condition — scale of the problem
British Heart Foundation · OHID estimates
Roughly half of adults with hypertension are either undiagnosed or diagnosed but not achieving their blood pressure target. The absence of symptoms until damage is established is what makes routine monitoring clinically significant.
Research · treatment evidence
Key finding
−20%
reduction in coronary heart disease risk
per 10 mmHg fall in systolic blood pressure · Lancet 2016 · 613,815 participants
Ettehad — blood pressure-lowering meta-analysis
The Lancet · 2016 · 123 trials · n=613,815
Every 10 mmHg reduction in systolic blood pressure (the top reading) reduced coronary heart disease by 17%, stroke by 27%, heart failure by 28%, and all-cause mortality by 13%. Major cardiovascular events fell by approximately 20% overall.
Key finding
−25%
reduction in major cardiovascular events
targeting systolic <120 mmHg vs <140 mmHg · NEJM 2015 · n=9,361
SPRINT — intensive vs standard BP targets
NEJM · 2015 · n=9,361 · stopped early for benefit
An intensive systolic target (below 120 mmHg) reduced composite cardiovascular events by 25% and all-cause mortality by 27% compared with a standard target (below 140 mmHg). The trial was stopped early on efficacy grounds.
Research · lifestyle impact
Key finding
−14 mmHg
fall in systolic blood pressure
DASH-style eating in hypertensive adults · NEJM 2001 · n=412
DASH-Sodium — dietary pattern trial
Sacks et al · NEJM · 2001 · n=412
A DASH-style dietary pattern (fruit, vegetables, whole grains, lean protein, low saturated fat) dropped systolic blood pressure by up to 14 mmHg — a reduction comparable to single-drug therapy and achievable through diet alone.
Key finding
~1 mmHg
blood pressure reduction per kilogram of weight lost
sustained weight reduction · Cochrane review · 2021
Weight loss and blood pressure — Cochrane review
Semlitsch et al · Cochrane Database · 2021
Sustained weight reduction lowers blood pressure by around 1 mmHg per kilogram of weight lost. Salt reduction, regular aerobic activity, and alcohol moderation each produce further independent reductions — and effects are additive.
How high blood pressure damages — part 1 of 2
💪
Arterial wall stress
Constant high pressure damages the endothelium (inner vessel lining) — accelerates plaque build-up and progressively stiffens vessels.
Atherosclerosis
🫀
Left ventricular overload
The heart muscle thickens (left ventricular hypertrophy — LVH) pumping against sustained high pressure — eventually weakening and failing.
Heart failure
🧠
Small-vessel brain disease
Tiny deep brain vessels fray under pressure — microbleeds and white-matter lesions (areas of brain damage) accumulate silently.
Vascular dementia
How high blood pressure damages — part 2 of 2
🫘
Glomerular hypertension
Pressure is transmitted to the kidney's filtering units (glomeruli — microscopic filtration structures) — progressive scarring develops (nephrosclerosis).
Kidney failure
👁️
Retinal microvessel damage
Retinal arterioles narrow, leak, and haemorrhage — detectable on eye examination long before any visual symptoms appear.
Vision loss
⚡
Sympathetic overdrive
Chronic sympathetic nervous system (fight-or-flight) activation drives vasoconstriction (vessel narrowing) and kidney sodium retention — a self-reinforcing cycle.
BP spiral
