Hypertension
— the silent killer
What high blood pressure is, why it matters, and what the evidence says works.
Dr Paul · Retired NHS GP · helf.school

Why it matters: five body systems
❤️
Heart
Coronary artery disease, heart attack, heart failure. Every 20 mmHg rise doubles cardiovascular mortality.
2×
🧠
Brain
Leading modifiable risk factor for stroke. Also drives vascular dementia through small-vessel damage.
Stroke
🫘
Kidneys
Second-leading cause of kidney failure in the UK after diabetes. Often silent until advanced.
2nd
👁️
Eyes
Hypertensive retinopathy — damaged retinal vessels, haemorrhages, and permanent vision loss.
Retina
🩸
Arteries
Accelerates atherosclerosis, triggers aortic aneurysms, drives peripheral arterial disease.
PAD
What your doctor might do
🩺
Clinic BP measurement
Multiple readings over several visits — single high readings alone don't confirm a diagnosis.
→ Check
📊
Ambulatory / home monitoring
24-hour ABPM or home readings confirm the diagnosis — NICE NG136 recommends this before treatment.
→ Confirm
🩸
Blood and urine tests
Kidney function, cholesterol, blood glucose, urine albumin — screens for causes and consequences.
→ Test
💓
ECG
Looks for left ventricular hypertrophy (thickened heart muscle) — a sign of long-standing strain.
→ Scan
🌿
Lifestyle guidance
Salt reduction, weight loss, physical activity, alcohol moderation, DASH-style eating pattern.
→ Advise
💊
Medications (NICE pathway)
ACE inhibitor / ARB, calcium channel blocker, thiazide — often combined. Specialist referral if resistant.
→ Treat
Research · the UK picture
1 in 4
UK adults have high blood pressure
Scale of hypertension in the UK
British Heart Foundation · NHS Digital Health Survey for England
Roughly 14 million people across England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland. Prevalence rises steeply with age — more than half of over-65s are affected.
~50%
undiagnosed or poorly controlled
The "silent killer" problem
British Heart Foundation · PHE / OHID estimates
Roughly half of adults with hypertension are either undiagnosed or diagnosed but not reaching target. Symptoms rarely appear until damage is advanced.
Research · treatment evidence
−20%
CHD · −27% stroke · per 10 mmHg ↓
Ettehad · BP-lowering meta-analysis
The Lancet · 2016 · 123 trials · n=613,815
Every 10 mmHg reduction in systolic BP reduced major cardiovascular events by ~20%, coronary heart disease by 17%, stroke by 27%, heart failure by 28%, and all-cause mortality by 13%.
−25%
CV events · target <120 vs <140
SPRINT — intensive vs standard
NEJM · 2015 · n=9,361 · stopped early for benefit
Intensive target (systolic <120 mmHg) cut composite cardiovascular events by 25% and all-cause mortality by 27% compared with standard (<140). Trial stopped early on efficacy.
Research · lifestyle impact
−8 to −14
mmHg · DASH diet · hypertensive adults
DASH-Sodium — dietary pattern trial
Sacks et al · NEJM · 2001 · n=412
DASH-style eating (fruit, vegetables, whole grains, lean protein, low saturated fat) dropped systolic BP by up to 14 mmHg — a reduction comparable to single-drug therapy.
~1 mmHg
per kg lost · sustained weight reduction
Weight loss · Cochrane review
Semlitsch et al · Cochrane Database · 2021
Sustained weight reduction lowers BP by around 1 mmHg per kg lost. Salt reduction, regular aerobic activity, and alcohol moderation each add further independent reductions.
How high BP actually damages
💪
Arterial wall stress
Constant high pressure damages the endothelium (inner lining) — accelerates plaque build-up and stiffens vessels.
→ Atherosclerosis
🫀
Left ventricular overload
The heart thickens (LVH) pumping against high pressure — eventually weakens and fails.
→ Heart failure
🧠
Small-vessel brain disease
Tiny deep vessels fray under pressure — microbleeds and white-matter lesions accumulate silently.
→ Vascular dementia
🫘
Glomerular hypertension
Pressure transmitted to the kidney's filtering units (glomeruli) scars them — nephrosclerosis develops.
→ Kidney failure
👁️
Retinal microvessel damage
Retinal arterioles narrow, leak and haemorrhage — detectable on eye examination long before symptoms.
→ Vision loss
⚡
Sympathetic overdrive
Chronic sympathetic (fight-or-flight) activation drives vasoconstriction and kidney sodium retention — a self-reinforcing loop.
→ BP spiral
