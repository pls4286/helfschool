Why gallstones form — the 5 Fs (1 of 2)
♀️
Female
Oestrogen raises cholesterol in bile and slows gallbladder contractions — both promote stone formation. Women are around twice as likely to develop gallstones as men.
2×
🎂
Forty
Risk increases steadily with age as bile composition shifts and gallbladder motility — emptying ability — declines.
↑ age
🤰
Fertile — pregnant
Pregnancy amplifies oestrogen and progesterone effects — bile becomes more cholesterol-saturated and the gallbladder empties more slowly.
↑ 3×
Why gallstones form — the 5 Fs (2 of 2)
⚖️
Fat — overweight
Excess body fat increases cholesterol output in bile. Rapid weight loss also raises risk by causing cholesterol supersaturation — the bile becomes unstable.
BMI ↑
🧬
Fair — and family history
Gallstones run in families. A first-degree relative with gallstones — parent, sibling, or child — increases personal risk substantially.
↑ FH
When gallstones cause problems — complications (1 of 2)
😣
Biliary colic
Severe cramping upper right abdominal pain — a stone temporarily blocks the cystic duct. Typically builds over minutes and lasts 30 minutes to several hours, then subsides. Often triggered by a fatty meal.
30m+
🤒
Acute cholecystitis
Prolonged blockage inflames the gallbladder — persistent pain, fever, and Murphy's sign (tenderness on deep inspiration). Clinically recognised as requiring hospital assessment. NICE CG188 recommends surgery within 1 week.
1 wk
When gallstones cause problems — complications (2 of 2)
💛
Choledocholithiasis — stone in the common bile duct
Bile backs up causing jaundice — yellow skin and eyes, pale stools, dark urine. ERCP (a camera procedure through the mouth) is used to clear the duct.
CBD
⚠️
Cholangitis + pancreatitis
Charcot's triad — pain, fever, and jaundice together — suggests bile duct infection. Clinically recognised as time-sensitive — 111 and urgent GP services exist for this. Gallstone pancreatitis ranges from mild to life-threatening.
Urgent
Diagnosis and treatment — what your doctor does (1 of 2)
🗣️
History and 5 Fs assessment
Pain character, duration, timing. Fever, jaundice, dark urine. Risk factors including BMI, pregnancies, family history, and medications.
Hx
🩸
Blood tests — LFTs, CRP, amylase
Raised bilirubin and ALP suggest bile duct involvement. Raised amylase or lipase suggests pancreatic involvement — guides the next imaging step.
LFTs
🔊
Ultrasound — first-line imaging
Non-invasive, no radiation. Detects gallbladder stones and features of cholecystitis well. Less sensitive for small stones in the common bile duct.
US
Diagnosis and treatment — what your doctor does (2 of 2)
🧲
MRCP if common bile duct stones suspected
MRI-based imaging of the bile and pancreatic ducts — detailed, non-invasive. Has replaced diagnostic ERCP for most patients. If a stone is confirmed, ERCP clears the duct.
MRI
🔪
Laparoscopic cholecystectomy
Keyhole gallbladder removal — typically a day case. Standard treatment for symptomatic gallstones. NICE CG188 recommends within 1 week for acute cholecystitis. People eat normally afterwards.
Day

C-GALL RCT — surgery vs watchful waiting
Health Technology Assessment · NIHR 2024 · Innes K, Ahmed I et al.
Around half of adults randomised to watchful waiting crossed over to surgery within 24 months — due to ongoing symptoms or complications. Condition-specific quality of life significantly favoured cholecystectomy ⚑.
Key Finding

C-GALL — quality of life outcomes
Health Technology Assessment · NIHR 2024 · p<0.001
Surgery significantly better for disease-specific outcomes despite no significant difference in overall pain scores. Both approaches are clinically acceptable — but most watchful-waiting patients eventually chose surgery ⚑.
NICE CG188 — three key recommendations
⏸️
Asymptomatic
No treatment needed for silent gallstones
NICE recommends reassurance only for asymptomatic gallstones found in a normal gallbladder. Most will never cause problems — watchful waiting is the appropriate approach. No surgery referral unless symptoms develop.
NICE CG188 · Rec 1.1.1 · Confirmed current 2025
🔪
Symptomatic
Offer keyhole surgery for symptomatic gallstones
Laparoscopic cholecystectomy is the recommended treatment for symptomatic gallstone disease — biliary colic or cholecystitis. Day-case surgery is standard. People eat normally after successful treatment.
NICE CG188 · Rec 1.2.2
⏰
Acute cholecystitis
Surgery within 1 week of diagnosis
For acute cholecystitis, NICE identifies early laparoscopic cholecystectomy — within 1 week — as a key priority. Earlier surgery reduces risk of recurrent episodes and complications while waiting.
NICE CG188 · Key priority rec 1.2.4
