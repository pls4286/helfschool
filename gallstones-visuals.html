When gallstones cause problems
Complications — what can go wrong
Most gallstones stay silent. These are the complications that arise when stones block bile ducts.
😣
Biliary colic
Severe cramping upper right abdominal pain — stone temporarily blocks the cystic duct. Typically lasts 30 min–several hours then subsides.
30m+
🤒
Acute cholecystitis
Prolonged blockage inflames the gallbladder — persistent pain, fever, and Murphy's sign (tenderness on deep breath). Requires hospital assessment and surgery within 1 week (NICE CG188).
1 wk
💛
Choledocholithiasis + jaundice
Stone passes into the common bile duct — bile backs up causing yellow skin and eyes, pale stools, dark urine. Requires ERCP to clear the duct.
CBD
⚠️
Cholangitis + pancreatitis
Charcot's triad (pain + fever + jaundice) suggests bile duct infection — clinically time-sensitive. 111 and urgent GP services exist for this. Gallstone pancreatitis can range from mild to life-threatening.
999↑
What your doctor does
Diagnosis and treatment pathway
In UK practice — Source: NICE CG188
🗣️
History + 5 Fs assessment
Pain character, duration, timing; fever, jaundice, dark urine; risk factors including BMI, pregnancies, family history, medications.
Hx
🩸
Blood tests — LFTs + CRP + amylase
Raised bilirubin / ALP (alkaline phosphatase) → bile duct involvement. Raised amylase / lipase → pancreatic involvement.
LFTs
🔊
Ultrasound — first-line imaging
Non-invasive, no radiation; detects gallbladder stones and features of cholecystitis. Less sensitive for small CBD stones.
US
🧲
MRCP if CBD stones suspected
MRI-based imaging of the bile and pancreatic ducts — detailed, non-invasive; replaced diagnostic ERCP for most patients.
MRI
🔪
Laparoscopic cholecystectomy
Keyhole gallbladder removal — typically day-case. Standard treatment for symptomatic gallstones; NICE recommends within 1 week for acute cholecystitis.

The research
Surgery vs watchful waiting — the C-GALL trial
Source: C-GALL RCT · Health Technol Assess · 2024 · DOI: 10.3310/MNBY3104
UK RCT · 2024
C-GALL: cholecystectomy vs conservative management
Key finding
~50%
crossed over to surgery within 2 years
of those assigned to watchful waiting — due to symptoms or complications
Adults randomised 1:1 to surgery or watchful waiting, 24 months follow-up. No significant difference in overall quality of life — but condition-specific quality of life significantly favoured surgery (mean difference 9.0, p<0.001).
NIHR Health Technology Assessment · Innes K, Ahmed I et al.

NICE CG188 — what UK guidelines say
Three key clinical recommendations
NICE Clinical Guideline CG188 · Published 2014 · Confirmed current 2025
⏸️
Asymptomatic
No treatment needed for silent gallstones
NICE recommends reassurance only for asymptomatic gallstones found in a normal gallbladder. Most will never cause problems — watchful waiting is the appropriate approach. No surgery referral is needed unless symptoms develop.
NICE CG188 · Rec 1.1.1
🔪
Symptomatic
Offer keyhole surgery for symptomatic gallstones
Laparoscopic cholecystectomy is the recommended treatment for symptomatic gallstone disease (biliary colic or cholecystitis). Day-case surgery is the standard approach. People can eat normally after successful treatment.
NICE CG188 · Rec 1.2.2
⏰
Acute cholecystitis
Surgery within 1 week of diagnosis
For acute cholecystitis (inflamed gallbladder), NICE identifies early laparoscopic cholecystectomy — within 1 week — as a key priority. Earlier surgery reduces risk of recurrent episodes and complications during the waiting period.
NICE CG188 · Key priority rec 1.2.4

What to take away from this
Four things worth remembering about gallstones
🔇
Most gallstones are silent — and stay that way
Around 80% of people with gallstones never develop symptoms. An incidental finding on a scan is not a reason to panic.
80%
😣
Biliary colic is the signal that stones are causing problems
Severe upper right abdominal pain after a fatty meal — lasting 30 minutes or more — is the classic presentation. Worth reporting.
30m+
⚠️
Pain + fever + jaundice together is a recognised red flag
Charcot's triad suggests bile duct infection — clinically time-sensitive. 111 and urgent GP services exist for this combination.
Urgent
🔪
Keyhole surgery is safe, effective, and usually day-case
The C-GALL trial confirms surgery produces better disease-specific outcomes — and around half of those who choose watchful waiting end up having it anyway.
