Myth vs Evidence
Common misconceptions
Two beliefs that don't reflect the clinical picture
🌞
"A tan shows your skin is healthy"
The evidence
A tan is the skin's visible response to DNA damage caused by UV radiation. The evidence shows UV — from sun and sunbeds — causes around 86% of UK melanoma cases. There is no such thing as a safe tan.
🔬
"Melanoma only affects people with fair skin"
The evidence
Melanoma occurs across all skin tones. Acral lentiginous melanoma — developing on the palms, soles, and under the nails — is more frequent in people with darker skin and can be harder to spot.
What is it? · 1 of 2
Melanoma — the basics
A cancer of the skin's pigment cells
🔬
What melanoma is
Melanoma arises from the skin's pigment cells. Its danger comes from the ability to spread to lymph nodes and distant organs if not caught early.
🔡
The ABCDE rule
Asymmetry · irregular Border · multiple Colours · Diameter over 6mm · Evolution — any change. A mole that looks different also warrants assessment.
📋
Four main subtypes
Superficial spreading (most common) · Nodular (fast-growing) · Lentigo maligna (sun-damaged skin) · Acral lentiginous (palms, soles, nails — more common in darker skin).
What is it? · 2 of 2
Risk factors and gene testing
UV is the biggest modifiable risk
☀️
UV exposure — the key risk
Intense UV — sunbeds and strong sunshine — carries more melanoma risk than everyday exposure. Fair skin, many moles, and family history are risk factors.
🧬
The BRAF gene mutation
Around half of melanomas carry a BRAF mutation. NICE recommends testing at diagnosis for higher-risk disease, as the result determines which treatments are available.
📊
Stage at diagnosis matters most
Staged 1 to 4 based on tumour depth, lymph nodes, and spread. Stage at diagnosis is the single most important factor in determining outcome.
Why does it matter?
The scale — and the stage gap
Survival depends heavily on stage at diagnosis
📈
A rising diagnosis rate
Around 18,300 melanoma diagnoses in the UK each year — roughly 48 every day. Rates have risen by around 31% over the past decade.
🟢
Stage 1 — around 100% five-year survival
Stage 1 melanoma has around 100% five-year survival. This falls to 85% at stage 2 and 75% at stage 3, where lymph nodes are involved.
💊
Modern treatment changed the outlook
Fewer than half survived 10 years in the 1970s. By 2018, more than 9 in 10 did — driven by immunotherapy and gene-targeted treatment.
What your doctor might do · 1 of 2
Diagnosis and staging
From assessment to full staging
🔍
Dermatoscopy and biopsy
Lesions are assessed using dermatoscopy — a handheld magnifier. If uncertain, a biopsy removes and examines the lesion, confirming diagnosis and measuring tumour depth.
🧬
BRAF testing at diagnosis
NICE recommends BRAF testing at diagnosis for stage IIA and above. Early BRAF status means gene-targeted treatment can begin promptly if needed.
🏥
Sentinel node and staging scans
Sentinel lymph node biopsy checks whether cancer has reached the nearest nodes. CT, PET-CT, and MRI then assess spread and establish the stage.
What your doctor might do · 2 of 2
Treatment pathways
Surgery, immunotherapy, and targeted treatment
🏥
Surgery — mainstay for early disease
Surgical removal with a defined margin is the primary treatment for early disease. For most stage 1 and stage 2 cases, surgery is curative.
💊
Immunotherapy — activating immune response
Immunotherapy activates the immune system to recognise and attack cancer cells. Checkpoint inhibitors, given alone or in combination, have transformed treatment of advanced melanoma.
🧬
Gene-targeted therapy for BRAF disease
Targeted oral tablets block the protein driving BRAF-mutant melanoma growth. Used in advanced disease and as adjuvant treatment after surgery for stage III.

Two trials that redefined survival in advanced melanoma

KEYNOTE-006 — immunotherapy more than doubled survival

A checkpoint inhibitor immunotherapy was compared with an older immune treatment in advanced melanoma. Immunotherapy more than doubled median survival — 32.7 months versus 15.9 months — and is now a standard first-line treatment.

Robert C et al. Lancet Oncol 2019 · PMID 31345627
5-Year Overall Survival
34%
with gene-targeted therapy in BRAF-mutant metastatic melanoma
BRAF V600-mutant disease · COMBI-d and COMBI-v pooled
COMBI trials — 34% alive at 5 years

A pooled analysis of two large trials showed 34% of patients with BRAF-mutant metastatic melanoma alive at 5 years on gene-targeted combination treatment. Before the treatment era, very few patients with advanced disease survived this long.

Robert C et al. N Engl J Med 2019 · PMID 31166680

CheckMate 067 — 52% alive at 5 years

Combination immunotherapy — blocking two immune-suppression pathways simultaneously — was compared with an older single immune treatment in advanced melanoma. At 5 years, 52% of patients on combination treatment were alive, versus 26% on the older treatment alone.

Larkin J et al. N Engl J Med 2019 · PMID 31562797
