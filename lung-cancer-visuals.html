Myth vs Evidence
What social media gets wrong about lung cancer
Two persistent claims — and what published evidence actually shows
📱 The claim
"Lung cancer only happens to smokers. If you've never smoked, there's nothing to worry about."
✓ The evidence
Around 15% of UK lung cancer diagnoses are in people who have never smoked. Radon gas, asbestos, air pollution, and inherited genetic risk all play a role — and never-smoker lung cancer would rank 8th in the UK cancer death table if counted separately.
Khan et al. BJC Reports 2023 · PMID 39516402
📱 The claim
"Stage 4 lung cancer is a death sentence. There's nothing medicine can offer."
✓ The evidence
Immunotherapy and targeted therapy have transformed outcomes for specific groups. In EGFR-mutated lung cancer, targeted therapy extended median survival to 38.6 months. In high PD-L1 disease, immunotherapy nearly doubled progression-free survival compared with chemotherapy.
Ramalingam et al. NEJM 2020 · Reck et al. NEJM 2016
Section 1 · What is it? (1 of 2)
What is lung cancer?
A disease of the airways — with two very different types
🫁
A cancer that starts in the airways or lung tissue
Lung cancer begins when cells in the airways or lung tissue divide out of control. The resulting tumour can spread through the bloodstream or lymphatic system to other parts of the body.
🔬
Non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) — 80–85% of cases
The most common form, with three main subtypes — adenocarcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma, and large cell carcinoma. NSCLC grows more slowly than SCLC and is more amenable to surgery and targeted treatment.
⚡
Small cell lung cancer (SCLC) — 15–20% of cases
Almost always linked to smoking, SCLC grows and spreads very rapidly and has usually already spread beyond the lung by the time of diagnosis. Surgery is rarely possible; chemotherapy is the main treatment.
Section 1 · What is it? (2 of 2)
Risk factors and molecular testing
Why modern treatment starts with testing the tumour's biology
🚭
Smoking — the single biggest risk factor
72% of UK cases are caused by smoking. Adding radon gas, asbestos, and air pollution, 79% of all cases are considered preventable.
Cancer Research UK · cancerresearchuk.org
🌍
Not only a smoker's disease
Around 15% of diagnoses are in people who have never smoked. Risk factors include radon gas, asbestos, air pollution, and inherited genetic risk.
Khan et al. BJC Reports 2023 · PMID 39516402
🧬
Every NSCLC tumour is molecularly tested
Every NSCLC tumour is tested for EGFR mutations and PD-L1 expression. Results determine whether targeted therapy, immunotherapy, or chemotherapy is the right approach.
NICE NG122 · Reck et al. NEJM 2016
Section 2 · Why does it matter?
The scale — and the opportunity
Why earlier diagnosis changes everything
📊
~49,300 new cases per year — the third most common UK cancer
Lung cancer accounts for 12% of all new UK cancer cases. Incidence is falling in men but still rising in women.
Cancer Research UK · cancerresearchuk.org
⚠️
The UK's biggest cancer killer — ~32,800 deaths per year
The UK's biggest cancer killer — 19% of all cancer deaths. A 10-year survival of 11.1% reflects how often it is caught late.
Cancer Research UK · cancerresearchuk.org
🎯
Stage 1 five-year survival: almost 65%
Stage 1 five-year survival is almost 65%, versus 11.1% overall. The NHS is rolling out targeted CT scanning for high-risk individuals across England.
Cancer Research UK · cancerresearchuk.org
Section 3 · What your doctor might discuss (1 of 2)
Getting to a diagnosis
From first symptoms to confirmed staging
🔍
Symptoms that prompt urgent investigation
NICE NG122 flags persistent cough, blood in cough, breathlessness, chest pain, weight loss, hoarseness, and recurrent chest infections as reasons for urgent assessment.
NICE NG122 · Lung cancer: diagnosis and management, 2019
🏥
From chest X-ray to CT, PET scan, and biopsy
A chest X-ray, then CT scan and PET-CT to check for spread. A tissue biopsy confirms cancer type and enables molecular testing.
NICE NG122 · Lung cancer: diagnosis and management, 2019
📋
Staging — the single most important factor
Staged 1–4 by tumour size, lymph node involvement, and organ spread. Stage at diagnosis is the most important factor in determining treatment.
NICE NG122 · Lung cancer: diagnosis and management, 2019
Section 3 · What your doctor might discuss (2 of 2)
Treatment — more options than ever before
Modern lung cancer treatment is matched to the biology of each tumour
⚕️
Surgery and precision radiotherapy
For early-stage NSCLC, lobectomy offers the best chance of cure. Where surgery is unsuitable, SABR — precision radiotherapy — delivers excellent outcomes.
NICE NG122 · Lung cancer: diagnosis and management, 2019
🛡️
Immunotherapy for high PD-L1 tumours
For 23–28% of advanced NSCLC with high PD-L1, a checkpoint inhibitor is now standard first-line treatment — with better survival than chemotherapy.
Reck et al. NEJM 2016 · PMID 27718847
🎯
Targeted therapy for EGFR-mutated tumours
For NSCLC with an EGFR mutation — around 10–15% of cases — a targeted oral tablet is standard first-line treatment with improved survival.
Ramalingam et al. NEJM 2020 · PMID 31751012
Section 4 · What the research shows (1 of 2)
Screening saves lives — and immunotherapy works
Key Finding

high-risk smokers · 10-year follow-up · NELSON trial
NELSON — the trial behind NHS screening

15,789 long-term smokers were randomly assigned to regular low-dose CT scans or no screening and followed for 10 years. The screened group had 24% fewer lung cancer deaths, with very few false alarms. This is the evidence that led the NHS to begin rolling out targeted lung cancer screening for high-risk individuals in England.

de Koning HJ et al. N Engl J Med 2020;382(6):503–513 · PMID 31995683
Key Finding
10.3
months
median PFS / immunotherapy vs 6.0 months on chemotherapy
high PD-L1 NSCLC (≥50%) · first-line · KEYNOTE-024
KEYNOTE-024 — immunotherapy replaces chemotherapy

Patients with advanced NSCLC and high PD-L1 expression were randomly assigned to immunotherapy or standard chemotherapy. Those on immunotherapy went 10.3 months before their cancer grew — versus 6.0 months on chemotherapy — and were 40% less likely to die. Immunotherapy became the new standard first-line treatment for this group.

Reck M et al. N Engl J Med 2016;375(19):1823–1833 · PMID 27718847
Section 4 · What the research shows (2 of 2)
Targeted therapy — matching treatment to the tumour
Key Finding
38.6

EGFR-mutated advanced NSCLC · FLAURA trial
FLAURA — a newer targeted tablet outperforms its predecessor

Patients with EGFR-mutated advanced lung cancer were randomly assigned to a newer third-generation EGFR inhibitor or the previous standard EGFR tablets, with overall survival as the primary endpoint. The newer drug extended median survival to 38.6 months versus 31.8 months — nearly 7 months longer — also showed better protection against spread to the brain, and is now the preferred first-line treatment for EGFR-mutated advanced NSCLC.

Ramalingam SS et al. N Engl J Med 2020;382(1):41–50 · PMID 31751012
