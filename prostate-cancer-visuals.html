Prostate
Cancer
What medicine knows — and what the evidence shows about treatment, screening, and survival.
Dr Paul · Retired NHS GP · helf.school
The scale of prostate cancer in the UK
~57,900
new cases per year
The most common cancer in UK males — 28% of all new male cancer cases. Around 160 diagnoses every day.
Cancer Research UK · 2019/2021–2022
78.9%
survive 10 or more years
Around 8 in 10 men diagnosed in the UK now survive 10 years or more — up from fewer than 1 in 4 in the early 1970s.
Cancer Research UK · 2018
⚠️
Around 12,200 deaths per year — 2nd most common cause of cancer death in UK males. Lifetime risk: 1 in 6 UK males diagnosed.
What is prostate cancer?
🫀
The prostate gland
Walnut-sized gland sitting below the bladder — surrounds the urethra and produces fluid that forms part of semen.
Anatomy
🔬
Cancer development
Cells divide and grow uncontrolled. Almost all are adenocarcinomas — gland-cell cancers. Most grow slowly; some are more aggressive.
>99%
📊
ISUP grade groups — how aggressive?
Cancer cells graded using Gleason score → translated into ISUP groups 1–5. Group 1 = least aggressive. Group 5 = most aggressive.
1 → 5
📍
Localised disease
Cancer confined within the prostate gland. The most treatable stage — surgery or radiotherapy can be curative.
Stage 1–2
🗺️
Locally advanced
Cancer has grown beyond the prostate capsule into surrounding tissue, but has not spread to distant sites.
Stage 3
🌐
Metastatic
Spread to bone, lymph nodes, or other distant sites. Manageable — not curable. Hormone therapy and chemotherapy extend survival.
Stage 4
Risk factors and the PSA test
⏳
Age
The most significant risk factor. Risk rises sharply after 50. Most diagnoses are in men aged 70–74. Incidence is highest in men aged 75–79.
After 50
🧬
Ethnicity — Black men at higher risk
Clinically recognised higher incidence in Black men compared with White men — established in NICE NG131 clinical guidance.
↑ Risk
👨‍👧‍👦
Family history
First-degree relative (father/brother) with prostate cancer increases risk. BRCA gene mutations also raise risk.
↑ Risk
🚫
No established preventable causes
Prostate cancer is NOT clearly linked to any modifiable lifestyle risk factor — no diet, exercise, or alcohol pattern has been conclusively established.
Cannot prevent
🩸
The PSA test — a marker, not a diagnosis
PSA (prostate-specific antigen) blood test measures a protein from the prostate. An elevated level prompts investigation — but can also be raised by benign conditions.
Marker only
⚠️
~3 in 4 with raised PSA will not have cancer
Around 75% of men with elevated PSA do not have prostate cancer — an imperfect marker that prompts investigation, not a definitive diagnosis.
75% benign
How prostate cancer is diagnosed — NICE NG131
💊
Symptoms — often none
Urinary difficulty, blood in urine or semen, or bone pain may occur. Most prostate cancers are found through PSA testing or incidental finding, without symptoms.
Often silent
🩸
PSA test and urgent referral
PSA above age-specific threshold → urgent 2-week wait referral to urology. Around 3 in 4 elevated PSA results will not be cancer.
2WW referral
🔭
mpMRI — first-line investigation
Multiparametric MRI recommended by NICE NG131 before biopsy. Scores 1–5 on Likert scale — identifies suspicious areas and reduces unnecessary biopsies.
NICE NG131
🔬
Prostate biopsy
Tissue samples confirm diagnosis. Both transrectal and transperineal approaches are used in UK practice — NICE NG131 notes a shift towards transperineal.
Confirms
📊
Grading + staging scans
ISUP grade group determined from biopsy. CT and/or bone scan used to assess spread for higher-risk disease.
ISUP 1–5
🏥
MDT review — risk stratification
All prostate cancer cases discussed at multidisciplinary team meeting. Grade, stage, and PSA combined to risk-stratify and agree treatment plan.
MDT
Treatment approaches in UK practice — NICE NG131
👁️
Active surveillance
NICE-recommended for low-risk localised disease. Regular PSA tests, MRI, and repeat biopsy — treatment deferred unless progression detected.
NICE NG131
🔪
Surgery — radical prostatectomy
Removal of the entire prostate — robotic-assisted in most UK centres. Side effects include urinary leakage and erectile dysfunction (variable severity).
Curative
☢️
Radiotherapy
External beam or internal seeds (brachytherapy). Often combined with hormone therapy for higher-risk disease. Bowel and urinary side effects occur.
Curative
💉
Hormone therapy — suppressing testosterone
Suppresses testosterone to slow cancer growth. Given by regular injection or as a daily oral tablet. Long-term side effects include bone density loss and fatigue.
ADT
💊
Chemotherapy — for metastatic disease
Added to hormone therapy for fit men with newly diagnosed metastatic disease. STAMPEDE trial showed extended median survival vs hormone therapy alone.
+10 months
💊
Androgen synthesis inhibitor — advanced disease
A further hormone-suppressant treatment given alongside a low-dose steroid. NICE-approved for metastatic hormone-sensitive prostate cancer.
NICE TA493
Research · treatment and screening
Key Finding
2.7%
prostate cancer-specific mortality at 15 years / similar across monitoring, surgery, and radiotherapy
1,643 men · PSA-detected localised prostate cancer · ProtecT trial · P=0.53
ProtecT — localised prostate cancer
Hamdy FC et al · N Engl J Med · 2023 · PMID 36912538
1,643 UK men with PSA-detected localised prostate cancer followed for a median 15 years. Prostate cancer-specific mortality was low and similar across all three treatment groups. The key differences are in side effects, not survival — the choice between monitoring, surgery, and radiotherapy is a trade-off of effects, not mortality risk.
Key Finding
0.69%
prostate cancer mortality / PSA screening group vs 0.78% without screening
415,357 men · UK primary care · 15-year follow-up · CAP trial
CAP trial — PSA screening
Martin RM et al · JAMA · 2024 · PMID 38581198
The largest PSA screening trial ever conducted — 415,357 UK men aged 50–69 randomised to a single PSA test or standard care. At 15 years, prostate cancer mortality was significantly lower in the screened group. First UK trial evidence that a single PSA test reduces prostate cancer deaths — though the absolute difference is modest.
Research · advanced disease treatment
Median Overall Survival
81
months
median OS / chemotherapy + hormone therapy vs 71 months on hormone therapy alone
metastatic hormone-naïve prostate cancer · HR 0.78 · STAMPEDE trial
STAMPEDE — chemotherapy added to hormone therapy
James ND et al · The Lancet · 2016 · PMID 26719232
Large UK platform trial comparing hormone therapy alone against hormone therapy plus chemotherapy in men with high-risk or metastatic prostate cancer. Adding chemotherapy upfront extended median overall survival from 71 to 81 months in the metastatic group. This established early chemotherapy alongside hormone therapy as standard care for fit men with newly diagnosed metastatic prostate cancer.
