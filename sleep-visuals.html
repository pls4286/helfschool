Five ways poor sleep matters for your health
💔
Cardiovascular
U-shaped link to heart attack, stroke and cardiovascular death — lowest risk at around 7 hours.
Yin et al. · JAHA 2017
↑ Risk CVD
🩸
Metabolic
Short sleep reduces insulin sensitivity within days. U-shaped curve for type 2 diabetes risk.
Shan et al. · Diabetes Care 2015
↑ Risk T2DM
🧠
Brain & dementia
Persistent short sleep (≤6h) from midlife linked to higher dementia risk 25 years later.
Sabia et al. · Nature Communications 2021
↑ Risk +30%
😔
Mental health
Insomnia both causes and worsens depression and anxiety — a genuinely two-way link. Treating sleep helps mood.
NICE · clinical guidance
Two-way ↔
⏳
Mortality
Both short sleep (<7h) and long sleep (>8h) linked to higher all-cause mortality in the largest meta-analysis.
Cappuccio et al. · SLEEP 2010
↑ Mortality +12% / +30%
What your doctor can actually do about sleep
📔
Two-week sleep diary
Bedtime, time to fall asleep, night-time waking, rise time, and how rested you felt. Reveals patterns memory cannot.
Step 1
🫁
Screen for obstructive sleep apnoea (OSA)
Loud snoring + daytime sleepiness warrants screening. Tools: Epworth, STOP-Bang. CPAP treatment can be life-changing.
→ Screen
🧠
CBT-I — NICE first-line for chronic insomnia
Cognitive behavioural therapy for insomnia. Works better than sleeping tablets long-term. Face-to-face, group or digital (e.g. Sleepio).
1st-line
💊
Sleeping tablets — the honest picture
Z-drugs & benzodiazepines licensed up to 4 weeks. Long-term: tolerance, dependence, falls, fractures. Short-term use only.
≤ 4 wks
🌙
Menopause · shift work · older age
Each brings specific sleep challenges worth raising directly. HRT may help menopausal sleep disruption; shift work needs scheduling strategies.
→ Discuss
🩺
Look for treatable underlying causes
Thyroid problems, reflux, restless legs, chronic pain, medications — a proper assessment looks for these rather than just treating symptoms.
→ Assess
What the evidence actually shows
~7h
lowest-risk sleep duration
Yin et al. · sleep duration & cardiovascular disease
JAHA · 2017 · meta-analysis · 3.5M+ participants
U-shaped relationship: lowest cardiovascular and mortality risk at approximately 7 hours, rising on either side.
+12% / +30%
mortality risk
(short / long sleep)
Cappuccio et al. · all-cause mortality meta-analysis
SLEEP · 2010 · 16 cohort studies · 1.38M participants
Short sleep +12% higher death risk · long sleep +30% higher — both independently predict mortality ⚑.
7–8h
lowest-risk sleep for type 2 diabetes
Shan et al. · sleep & type 2 diabetes
Diabetes Care · 2015 · meta-analysis
U-shape: each hour less than 7h = +9% T2DM risk · each hour more than 8h = +14% T2DM risk.
+30%
dementia risk · persistent short sleep
Sabia et al. · Whitehall II · 25-year follow-up
Nature Communications · 2021
Persistent short sleep (≤6h) from age 50 onwards = ~30% higher dementia risk 25 years later — independent of other cardiovascular factors.
Lasting
CBT-I effects persist after treatment ends
van Straten et al. · CBT-I meta-analysis
Sleep Medicine Reviews · 2018 · 87 RCTs
CBT-I produces consistent improvements in falling asleep, staying asleep, and sleep quality — and unlike sleeping tablets, effects persist long after treatment ends. Basis for NICE first-line recommendation.
