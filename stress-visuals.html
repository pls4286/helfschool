Stress — the biology and the evidence
Where chronic stress reaches in the body — and what the research actually shows reliably helps.
Dr Paul · Retired NHS GP · helf.school
The one distinction that changes everything
⚡ Acute stress
⚡
Short. Resolves. Body resets.
A surge of adrenaline and cortisol (the main stress hormone) for a specific challenge — interview, argument, near-miss. Then it's over. The body returns to baseline.
✓ This is healthy. The system works exactly as designed.
🌀 Chronic stress
🌀
Sustained. Never resets. Damage accumulates.
The same response running for weeks, months, years. Cortisol stays elevated. Inflammation builds. Sleep fragments. The body never gets the "all clear."
⚠ Allostatic load — cumulative wear and tear on every system.
Five systems chronic stress reaches
❤️
Heart & arteries
Blood pressure rises. Arteries inflame. Heart-attack risk climbs — in the same league as the major traditional risk factors.
↑ RiskBP · CHD
🧠
Mind, sleep, mood
Sleep breaks down first. Memory slips. Concentration wavers. Anxiety and depression become far more likely.
Waking3am
🫁
Gut
Brain and gut are directly wired via the vagus nerve. Reflux, bloating and IBS (cramping, urgency) all worsen.
↑ FlaresIBS
🛡️
Immune system
Dampened immunity plus rising background inflammation. More colds. Slower healing. Flares of eczema and IBD.
↓ Defence↑ Inflam
🍷
Behaviour spiral
Stress pulls toward quick fixes — extra alcohol, missed walks, poor food. Each one feeds more stress. The spiral tightens.

What a GP can actually do about stress
👂
Listen — and look at the whole picture
Work. Home. Sleep. Alcohol. Exercise. Money. Relationships. Noting main concerns in advance means nothing gets missed in the time available.
Start here
📋
Screen for anxiety & depression — PHQ-9 + GAD-7
Two validated questionnaires, 2 minutes each. Identify if stress has crossed into a clinically treatable condition.
→ Screen
💬
NHS Talking Therapies — self-refer
CBT, mindfulness, counselling. Free on the NHS in England. Self-referral available — search "NHS Talking Therapies" + your postcode.
Free · NHS
💊
SSRIs — sertraline, fluoxetine
Genuinely effective for moderate-to-severe anxiety and depression. For when symptoms meet clinical threshold.
If threshold
🩺
Rule out physical mimics
Thyroid · anaemia · vitamin D · sleep apnoea — all masquerade as stress or amplify it. A simple blood panel catches most.

Part A · What chronic stress actually does to the body
Key Finding
~33%
of first heart-attack risk
attributed to psychosocial stress · 52 countries · consistent across all ethnic groups
INTERHEART — Psychosocial Factors & Heart Attack
Rosengren et al. · The Lancet · 2004 · 52 countries · 11,000+ cases
Psychosocial stress ranked alongside smoking, high blood pressure and cholesterol as one of the nine biggest drivers of first heart attack.
Key Finding

IPD-Work — Job Strain & Coronary Heart Disease
Kivimäki et al. · The Lancet · 2012 · 13 studies
High demands plus low control at work = 23% higher coronary heart disease risk. Toxic pattern: pressure you can't do anything about.
Key Finding

mechanism — imaged for the first time via PET-CT · Lancet 2017
Tawakol et al. — the amygdala–inflammation pathway
The Lancet · 2017 · Massachusetts General & Harvard
PET-CT traced the pathway: amygdala activity → bone marrow → inflammatory cells → artery wall → heart attacks and strokes. Perceived stress correlated with every step.
Part B · What reliably helps — evidence, not guesswork
Key Finding
~38%
reduction in anxiety symptoms
from regular exercise · any format · regularity matters most
Rebar et al. — physical activity & mental health
Health Psychology Review · 2015 · meta-meta-analysis
Regular movement reduced anxiety by ~38% and depression by a medium effect size. Any format works — walking, cycling, resistance, mixed.

NHS Talking Therapies — England-wide programme outcomes
NHS England Digital · 2024
Roughly half of people who complete a course are classified as recovered. Real-world data. Free. Self-referral. No GP letter needed.
Key Finding
3×
better response vs placebo
CBT for generalised anxiety disorder · large effect size · durable at follow-up
Carpenter et al. — CBT for anxiety-related disorders
Depression & Anxiety · 2018 · 41 placebo-controlled RCTs
CBT produced response rates roughly 3× higher than placebo. Large effect size for generalised anxiety disorder. Effects durable at follow-up.
Why these approaches actually work — the biology
🫁
Slow breathing — fastest acting
Extended out-breaths activate the vagus nerve — sending a measurable "stand down" signal from body to brain. 4 in, 6 out.
→ 1–2 min
🏃
Regular movement — multi-pathway
Lowers resting cortisol. Reduces inflammation. Improves sleep quality. Raises serotonin, dopamine and BDNF.
→ Multi
🧠
CBT & mindfulness — break the loop
Both interrupt the thought-emotion-body cycle that keeps chronic stress running. Train noticing the spiral early, before cortisol builds.
→ Pattern
🤝
Human connection — biologically active
Genuine social support measurably buffers the stress response — lower cortisol, lower inflammation, better cardiovascular outcomes.
→ Buffer
