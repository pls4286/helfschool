Chronic stress is not a character flaw. It's physiology that forgot how to switch off.

The one distinction that changes everything
⚡ Acute stress
⚡
Short. Resolves. Body resets.
A surge of adrenaline and cortisol (the main stress hormone) for a specific challenge — interview, argument, near-miss in traffic. Then it's over. The body returns to baseline.
✓ This is healthy. The system works exactly as designed.
🌀 Chronic stress
🌀
Sustained. Never resets. Damage accumulates.
The same response running for weeks, months, years. Cortisol stays elevated. Inflammation builds. Sleep fragments. Mood drops. The body never gets the "all clear."
⚠ Allostatic load — the cumulative wear and tear on every system in your body.
Five systems chronic stress reaches
❤️
Heart & arteries
↑ BP
Blood pressure rises. Arteries inflame. Heart-attack risk rises — in the same league as smoking.
🧠
Mind, sleep, mood
3am
Sleep breaks down first. Memory slips. Concentration wavers. Anxiety and depression become far more likely.
🫁
Gut
↑ IBS
Brain and gut are directly wired together via the vagus nerve. Reflux, bloating, and IBS (cramping, urgency) all worsen.
🛡️
Immune system
↓ defence
↑ inflam.
Dampened immunity plus rising background inflammation. More colds. Slower healing. Flares of eczema and IBD.
🍷
Behaviour spiral
↑ risk
Stress pulls toward quick fixes — extra alcohol, missed walks, poor food. Each one feeds more stress. The spiral tightens.
What a GP can actually do about stress
👂
The starting point
Listen — and look at the whole picture
Work. Home. Sleep. Alcohol. Exercise. Money. Relationships. Each amplifies or buffers stress. Write down your three main concerns before you go in — NHS appointments are short.
📋
Screen for
Anxiety & depression — PHQ-9 + GAD-7
Two validated questionnaires, 2 minutes each. Identify if stress has crossed into a clinically treatable condition where specific therapies work particularly well.
💬
Free · No GP letter needed
NHS Talking Therapies — self-refer
CBT, mindfulness, counselling — free on the NHS in England. Self-referral available. Search "NHS Talking Therapies" + your postcode. No appointment needed first.
💊
When symptoms meet clinical threshold
SSRIs — sertraline (Lustral), fluoxetine (Prozac)
Genuinely effective for moderate-to-severe anxiety and depression. Not for "a bit of stress" — for when symptoms meet threshold. Decisions are individualised and reversible.
🩺
Rule out physical mimics
Thyroid · Anaemia · Vitamin D · Sleep apnoea
These all masquerade as stress or amplify it — and a simple blood panel catches most of them. Part of any thorough assessment.
Research · Part A What chronic stress is actually doing to your body
~33%
of first heart-attack risk from psychosocial stress
INTERHEART — Psychosocial Factors & Heart Attack
Rosengren A et al. · The Lancet · 2004 · 52 countries · 11,000+ cases
Psychosocial stress ranked alongside smoking, high blood pressure, and cholesterol as one of the nine biggest drivers of first heart attack. Effect seen across every country and every ethnic group. This is not a soft finding.
+23%
higher coronary heart disease risk from job strain
IPD-Work — Job Strain & Coronary Heart Disease
Kivimäki M et al. · The Lancet · 2012 · 197,000 adults across 13 studies
High demands + low control at work = 23% higher coronary heart disease risk. The specific toxic pattern is pressure you can't do anything about. Not just a hard job — a hard job with no autonomy.

the mechanism — imaged for the first time
Tawakol A et al. — The Amygdala–Inflammation Pathway
The Lancet · 2017 · Massachusetts General Hospital & Harvard
PET-CT scans traced the pathway: amygdala (brain threat-centre) activity → bone marrow → inflammatory cells → artery wall inflammation → heart attacks and strokes. Perceived stress correlated with every step. Not vague. Not in your head. Imaged.
Research · Part B What reliably helps — so you can act on evidence, not guesswork
~38%
reduction in anxiety from regular exercise
Rebar AL et al. — Physical Activity & Mental Health ⚑
Health Psychology Review · 2015 · meta-meta-analysis
Regular movement reduced anxiety symptoms by ~38% and depression by a medium effect size. Any format works — walking, cycling, resistance, mixed. Regularity matters more than intensity. 30 minutes, 3–5 times a week, is evidence-based stress medicine.
~50%
recovery rate — NHS Talking Therapies completers
NHS Talking Therapies — England-wide Programme Outcomes
NHS England Digital · 2024 · 1.7 million+ referrals
Roughly half of people who complete a course are classified as recovered — anxiety and depression scores fall below clinical threshold. Real-world data, not selected trial samples. Free. Self-referral. No GP letter needed.
3× better
than placebo
CBT response rate for generalised anxiety
Carpenter JK et al. — CBT for Anxiety-Related Disorders ⚑
Depression & Anxiety · 2018 · 41 placebo-controlled RCTs
CBT (Cognitive Behavioural Therapy — structured therapy that trains you to notice and reshape unhelpful thought patterns) produced response rates roughly 3× higher than placebo for generalised anxiety disorder. Large effect size. Effects durable at follow-up. Available free via NHS Talking Therapies.
Why these approaches actually work — the biology
🫁
Slow breathing — fastest acting
Extended out-breaths activate the vagus nerve — sending a measurable "stand down" signal from body to brain. Works within 1–2 minutes. 4 in, 6 out. Free. Immediate.
🏃
Regular movement — multi-pathway
Lowers resting cortisol. Reduces inflammation. Improves sleep quality. Raises serotonin, dopamine, and BDNF (a protein that supports brain-cell growth and mood regulation).
🧠
CBT & mindfulness — break the loop
Both interrupt the thought-emotion-body cycle that keeps chronic stress running. Train you to notice the spiral early and step out before cortisol and inflammation build up.
🤝
Human connection — biologically active
Genuine social support measurably buffers the stress response — lower cortisol, lower inflammation, better cardiovascular outcomes. Isolation amplifies stress. Connection is medicine.
