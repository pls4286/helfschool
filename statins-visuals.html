How statins work — and who needs them

The mechanism

Statins block HMG-CoA reductase — the key enzyme in the liver's cholesterol-making process. With this enzyme inhibited, the liver produces less cholesterol and draws more LDL from the bloodstream, lowering circulating levels.

Each 1 mmol/L reduction in LDL reduces the risk of major cardiovascular events by approximately one fifth.

Who is offered a statin (NICE NG238)

✓ Anyone with established cardiovascular disease — regardless of cholesterol level

✓ Anyone with a 10-year QRISK3 score of 10% or above

✓ People aged 85+ where age alone warrants consideration

~ Those below 10% risk where lifestyle change has been insufficient

The treatment gap

Fewer than half of people with existing cardiovascular disease have their cholesterol treated to target. Better statin uptake could prevent tens of thousands of heart attacks and strokes per year in the UK alone.

The side effect story — what the evidence actually shows
CTT Collaboration — The Lancet 2022
Individual data from 150,000+ people across 23 trials. Statins caused only a small excess of mostly mild muscle pain. More than 90% of all muscle symptom reports were not caused by the statin itself. After year one, low/moderate intensity statins caused no significant increase in muscle symptoms vs placebo.
Cholesterol Treatment Trialists, Lancet 2022
2026 Lancet label analysis
Most commonly cited side effects on statin packaging — cognitive impairment, depression, sleep disturbance, peripheral neuropathy — are not supported by causal evidence from randomised trials.
CTT Collaboration, Lancet 2026
The Nocebo Effect — ASCOT-LLA Trial
41%
more muscle symptoms when patients knew they were taking a statin
When the same patients were blinded — unaware whether they were taking statin or placebo — muscle symptoms were virtually identical in both groups.
The expectation of harm leads to real, experienced symptoms — even when the drug is not the cause.
Gupta A et al. ASCOT-LLA, The Lancet 2017
What the research shows
CTT Collaboration — The Lancet 2010 (updated 2022, 2026)
26 randomised trials, 170,000+ participants. Each 1 mmol/L LDL reduction reduces major cardiovascular events by ~20%. Benefit holds across all ages, both sexes, regardless of starting cholesterol level.
Cholesterol Treatment Trialists, Lancet 2010
ASCOT-LLA — The Lancet 2017 (nocebo trial)
Blinded vs unblinded statin use in same patients. When unblinded: 41% more muscle symptoms. When blinded: no difference vs placebo. Definitive demonstration of nocebo effect in statin therapy.
Gupta A et al. Lancet 2017
CVDPREVENT Audit 2025
Only 57% of higher-risk patients have a current lipid-lowering prescription. Fewer than 50% of those with existing CVD treated to recommended targets. Represents one of the largest preventable disease burdens in the NHS.
NHS CVDPREVENT, 2025
💊
Understand your risk
Ask your GP for a QRISK3 assessment if you haven't had one. Know your number before making decisions about treatment.
🗣️
Have the conversation
If you've been offered a statin and you're hesitant — take this information to your GP. The decision should be shared, not assumed.
⚠️
Don't just stop
If you experience side effects, speak to your GP before stopping. Switching statin or adjusting dose often resolves problems.
