How statins work — and who gets them
🧬
The mechanism
Statins block HMG-CoA reductase, the rate-limiting enzyme in liver cholesterol synthesis. The liver compensates by pulling more LDL from the blood — circulating LDL falls. Each 1 mmol/L LDL drop cuts major cardiovascular events by ~22% (CTT meta-analysis).
📋
NICE NG238 — who is offered a statin
Anyone with established CVD (regardless of cholesterol), plus anyone with a 10-year QRISK3 ≥10%. Secondary prevention uses higher-intensity dose; primary prevention typically starts lower.

Side effects · what the trial data show
💪
Muscle pain — largely nocebo
CTT 2022 individual-data meta-analysis: over 90% of muscle symptom reports on statins were NOT caused by the drug itself.
>90%
🧠
Cognitive effects — no RCT signal
Cognitive impairment, sleep disturbance and depression are listed on packaging, but randomised trial evidence does not support a causal link.
No signal
🍬
New-onset diabetes
A small absolute increase in predisposed individuals — mostly accelerating a diabetes already coming. CV benefit still outweighs it.
~+0.1%/yr
🫀
Liver enzyme rise — usually mild
Small, usually reversible ALT rises. Severe liver injury is rare (< 1 in 10,000). Routine LFTs at baseline and 3 months.
~1%
⚠️
Serious myopathy — rare
Rhabdomyolysis is genuinely rare (< 1 in 10,000). Usually linked to interacting drugs (some antibiotics, antifungals, antivirals, grapefruit).
<1 / 10k
Benefits · what statins deliver
💉
Secondary prevention
Treating 10,000 patients with existing CVD for 5 years prevents around 1,000 major cardiovascular events.
1,000 / 10k
🛡️
Primary prevention (higher risk)
In higher-risk individuals without previous events, around 500 events prevented per 10,000 treated for 5 years.
500 / 10k
❤️
Heart-attack reduction
Roughly a quarter-reduction in major vascular events for each 1 mmol/L drop in LDL sustained over time.
−22%
🧠
Ischaemic stroke reduction
Statin therapy lowers ischaemic stroke rates proportionally to LDL reduction — meaningful in both sexes, all ages.
~−15%
🎯
All-cause mortality (higher risk)
In higher-risk groups, statin therapy also reduces overall mortality — not just cardiovascular events.
~−10%
Research · do they work?
−22%

CTT Collaboration — 26 statin trials
Baigent et al · The Lancet · 2010 · n>170,000
Largest individual-patient meta-analysis in cardiovascular medicine. Benefit held across age, sex, starting cholesterol, and baseline risk. Every additional 1 mmol/L drop delivers further protection.
Confirmed
low-to-moderate doses · no excess muscle pain after year 1
CTT — muscle symptoms analysis
The Lancet · 2022 · n=150,000+
Individual-patient data from 23 trials. Over 90% of muscle symptoms in statin users were not caused by the drug. Low/moderate-intensity dose — no significant excess vs placebo after the first year.
Research · the nocebo effect
+41%
muscle symptoms when patients know it's a statin
ASCOT-LLA — nocebo analysis
Gupta et al · The Lancet · 2017 · n≈10,000
When the same patients knew they were on a statin, muscle symptoms were 41% more common. When blinded, symptoms on statin and placebo were essentially identical — the expectation of harm can produce real symptoms.
Not
supported
cognitive / depression / sleep — RCT evidence
Package-insert side effects — evidence review
Multiple large RCTs · Cochrane reviews
Many of the most commonly-cited statin side effects — cognitive impairment, depression, sleep disturbance, peripheral neuropathy — are not supported by randomised evidence as being causally linked to statin therapy.

CVDPREVENT Audit — UK primary care
NHS / BHF · ongoing audit · 2024 data
Fewer than half of those with established CVD are at recommended cholesterol targets. Closing this gap could prevent tens of thousands of heart attacks and strokes per year in the UK.

Managing statin-intolerance — clinical pathways
NICE NG238 · BHF / ESC guidance
Many people labelled "statin intolerant" tolerate a different statin or a lower dose. Stopping unilaterally without review often leaves the cardiovascular risk untreated. A switch or dose adjustment is worth discussing first.
How statins actually protect
🧪
HMG-CoA reductase block
Statins inhibit the rate-limiting enzyme in cholesterol synthesis — the liver makes less cholesterol internally.
→ Less synthesis
📥
LDL receptor upregulation
The liver responds by pulling more LDL out of the bloodstream to compensate — circulating LDL falls.
→ Lower LDL
🛡️
Plaque stabilisation
Statin therapy thickens the fibrous cap over existing plaques, making them less rupture-prone.
→ Fewer events
🔥
Anti-inflammatory action
Statins reduce vascular inflammation markers including CRP — part of the benefit beyond LDL-lowering.
→ Less inflammation
🌊
Endothelial function
Improve nitric-oxide signalling in the artery wall — arteries relax and dilate more normally.
→ Healthier vessels
💫
Pleiotropic effects
Benefits appear even when LDL drop is matched — statins offer protection beyond cholesterol lowering alone.
→ Extra benefit
